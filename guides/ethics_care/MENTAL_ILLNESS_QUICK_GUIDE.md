# CRISIS QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
## Severe Mental Illness Protocol - Emergency Edition

**Print this. Laminate it. Keep it accessible.**

---

## PAGE 1: IS THIS A CRISIS? WHAT DO I DO FIRST?

### 🚨 STEP 1: IS THIS A CRISIS?

**YES if ANY of these:**
- [ ] Immediate danger to self or others
- [ ] Cannot recognize people/places
- [ ] No sleep 48+ hours + hyperactive/paranoid
- [ ] Giving away all possessions / major financial decisions
- [ ] No food/water/movement for 24+ hours
- [ ] Violence or threats
- [ ] Complete break from shared reality + unsafe behavior

**If YES → Go to STEP 2**  
**If UNSURE → Default to YES (false positive is safer)**

---

### ⚡ STEP 2: FIRST 10 MINUTES

**DO THIS IMMEDIATELY:**

1. **Get help** - Call Crisis Lead (name: _______________) OR any 2 Circle members
   
2. **Safety first** - Remove immediate dangers:
   - Weapons
   - Medications (overdose risk)
   - Car keys
   - Access to heights/water

3. **Call for backup if:**
   - Violence happening → 112 (Sweden) / local emergency
   - Suicide attempt → 112 + psychiatric emergency
   - Medical emergency → 112

4. **One person stays with node** - Calm, quiet, present

---

### 🗣️ WHAT TO SAY (Crisis Language)

**SAY:**
- "I hear you're terrified. You're safe."
- "I'm worried about your safety."
- "I'm staying with you."
- "You don't have to talk. I'm here."

**DON'T SAY:**
- "That's not real"
- "Calm down"
- "You're being difficult"
- Threats or arguments

**OFFER CHOICES:**
- "Water or tea?"
- "Sit or lie down?"
- "Light on or off?"

---

### 📋 STEP 3: WHO DECIDES?

**Decision Authority:**
- **Crisis Lead** has mandate to act (name: _______________)
- If unavailable: **Any 2 Circle members** can jointly activate protocol
- Major decisions reviewed by full Circle within 72 hours

**Crisis Lead can:**
- Activate separation
- Call professional help
- Use Ulysses Contract (if exists)
- Make safety decisions

---

### 📞 STEP 4: WHEN TO CALL PROFESSIONALS

**CALL IMMEDIATELY if:**
- Suicide attempt or plan
- Violence toward others
- Severe medical symptoms (seizure, overdose, dehydration)
- Completely unable to eat/drink for 48+ hours
- Psychosis + dangerous behavior

**Sweden contacts:**
- Emergency: **112**
- Psychiatric emergency: **Local hospital emergency room**
- Crisis hotline: **1177** (healthcare advice)
- Mind suicide prevention: **90101** or chat via mind.se

**Have ready:**
- Node's name, age, location
- Current symptoms
- Medications (if known)
- Ulysses Contract (if exists)

---

### ⚙️ STEP 5: USE THE DECISION TREE

**Follow this path:**

**Is death imminent?**  
→ YES: Call 112. Override all refusals.  
→ NO: Continue ↓

**Does node consent to help?**  
→ YES: Follow their preferences. Use professional help if requested.  
→ NO: Continue ↓

**Is there a Ulysses Contract?**  
→ YES: Follow it. Activate Crisis Lead. Document.  
→ NO: Continue ↓

**Can Circle safely contain?**  
→ YES: Activate Low-Capacity Mode (see Page 2). Call for Circle support.  
→ NO: Professional help required. Call psychiatric emergency or 112.

**Is node refusing everything?**  
→ If YES + danger: Override refusal. Call professional help. Document: "We chose life over autonomy."

## PAGE 2: DURING CRISIS - WHAT TO DO

### 🏠 LOW-CAPACITY MODE (When Circle is overwhelmed)

**ACTIVATE when:**
- Fewer than 5 members available
- Crisis lasting 30+ days
- Multiple crises at once
- Caregivers burning out

**CORE FUNCTIONS ONLY:**
1. **Safety** - Prevent death/severe harm
2. **Food** - One meal/day minimum
3. **Sleep** - Protect everyone's sleep
4. **Presence** - 10-min check-in daily

**Everything else pauses.**

Tell node: "We're in survival mode. We can give: safety, food, presence. We'll get through this."

---

### 🚪 SEPARATION PROTOCOL

**Use when:**
- Violence occurred
- Others' safety threatened
- Node needs low-stimulation space
- Behavioral red lines crossed (see below)

**HOW TO SEPARATE WITHOUT ABANDONMENT:**

**Before:**
- "This is for safety, not punishment"
- Name timeframe: "This will last [X hours/days]"
- Name who checks in: "[Person] will visit daily at [time]"
- Validate feelings: "I know this feels terrible"

**During:**
- Scheduled check-ins (same time daily)
- Same person each time if possible
- 10-15 min: "How are you? Food okay? I'll be back tomorrow at [time]."
- No lectures. Just presence.

**After:**
- Structured return (see Protocol §8.2)
- No punishment
- "I'm glad you're back. No pressure."

---

### 🔴 BEHAVIORAL RED LINES (Always intervene)

**Physical safety:**
- Threats of violence
- Weapons
- Stalking/following
- Blocking exits
- Physical harm attempts

**Relational:**
- Repeated accusations (3+ times/day)
- Harassment (any medium)
- Invading private spaces
- Creating climate of fear

**Collective:**
- Sabotaging shared resources
- Disrupting others' sleep/food/safety
- Theft/financial manipulation

**Consent:**
- Sexual boundary crossing
- Coercion
- Ignoring "no"

**Action:** Immediate separation + professional consultation + documentation

---

### 👥 CAREGIVER ROTATION

**LIMITS (enforce these):**
- Max 20 hours/week per person
- Max 3 consecutive days
- Mandatory 2-day break after 3 days
- Can call for relief anytime, no shame

**If limits exceeded:** System failure. Activate Low-Capacity Mode or transition care.

**Handover checklist:**
- [ ] What happened this shift?
- [ ] Node's current state
- [ ] Any changes/concerns
- [ ] Next person briefed
- [ ] Outgoing caregiver okay?

### 📝 DOCUMENTATION (Do this)

**During crisis, record:**
- Date/time
- What happened
- Who was present
- What was done
- Why decisions were made

**This protects:**
- Node (evidence for future care)
- Circle (legal protection)
- Future (learning for next time)

**Store:** Encrypted, limited access, GDPR-compliant

---

### ✅ AFTER CRISIS (First Week)

**Days 1-3:**
- No requirements
- Node can be silent, withdrawn
- Bridge person checks in daily
- Just presence, no pressure

**Days 4-7:**
- Gentle invitations (not requirements)
- Still no work expectations
- Ask: "What feels manageable?"

**Week 2:**
- Gradual return to shared tasks
- Adjust based on capacity
- Bridge person continues support

**Within 14 days:**
- Post-Crisis Debrief (Appendix D)
- What worked? What didn't?
- What did we learn?
- What repairs needed?

---

### 🔑 KEY PRINCIPLES TO REMEMBER

**Belonging vs Participation:**
- **Belonging** = unconditional (always)
- **Participation** = conditional on safety (can be paused)

**The Rule:**
- **Belief is free**
- **Behavior has limits**

**Core Truth:**
- Mental illness changes **capacity**, not **worth**

**When confused:**
- Choose safety over autonomy in moment
- Repair relationship after crisis
- Document everything
- Get external input when stuck
