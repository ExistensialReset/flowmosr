# CRISIS_PROTOCOL.md

**Status:** ACTIVE  
**Core Principle:** Crisis is Data, not Doomsday  
**Secondary Principle:** Speed without coercion, coordination without domination  

---

## PREAMBLE

Crises test not only resilience, but **response speed under stress**.

Flow responds through:
- Pre-committed reserves  
- Distributed sensing  
- Temporary coordination layers  

**Baseline protection overrides all other activities.**

All crisis actions are:
- Time-bound  
- Logged  
- Reviewed for systemic learning  

---

## §1. ACTIVATION

### 1.1 Automatic Trigger (Primary)

Crisis Mode activates automatically when:

- >15% of a Node lacks Baseline resources  
- OR critical infrastructure failure is detected  
- OR rapid degradation trend (>10% decline within 48h)

> No vote required for initial activation

---

### 1.2 Human Override (Secondary)

Members may:
- Escalate faster (manual trigger)
- De-escalate if false positive (≥60% agreement)

---

### 1.3 Psychological Signal

Crisis Mode may also activate if:

- ≥30% report fear / instability in Pulse Check  
- OR Canaries escalate twice within 7 days  

> Psychological collapse = real crisis

---

## §2. THE ESCALATION STACK

### LEVEL 1 — LOCAL (Immediate Response)

- Deploy pre-committed **Baseline Buffer (30%)**
- Activate **Crisis Roles (temporary, 24–72h max):**
  - Resource Mapper
  - Communication Anchor
  - Wellbeing Watch

- Automatic redistribution from shared pools (not individual property)

> No reliance on voluntary sharing at this stage

---

### LEVEL 2 — NODE (Stabilization)

- AI-assisted logistics modeling (if available)
- Scenario projections (best / worst / probable)
- Temporary resource reallocation from pre-agreed reserves

- Conflict monitoring activated:
  - Identify stress clusters early
  - Deploy mediators proactively

---

### LEVEL 3 — REGIONAL (Load Balancing)

- Inter-node redistribution using surplus mapping
- Regional reserves activated automatically
- Temporary transport / logistics prioritization

- External facilitators deployed if:
  - conflict spikes
  - coordination breaks down

---

### LEVEL 4 — BIOREGIONAL (System Coordination)

- Full transparency of supply chains
- Temporary coordination council (rotating, time-limited)
- Decisions must:
  - be logged
  - expire automatically unless renewed

---

### LEVEL 5 — EXISTENTIAL / FAILSAFE

- AI optional / disabled for dependency testing
- Manual coordination takes over
- Identify:
  - hidden fragilities
  - over-centralization risks

- Emergency simplification:
  - reduce system complexity
  - focus only on Baseline survival

---

## §3. RESOURCE LOGIC

### 3.1 Pre-Committed Pools

Before crisis, all Nodes maintain:

- Shared Baseline reserves (non-personal)
- Emergency allocation rules agreed in advance

> Crisis does NOT rely on spontaneous generosity

---

### 3.2 Personal Assets

- Remain voluntary  
- Never forcibly redistributed  

But:

- Social transparency applies  
- Hoarding patterns are visible (not punished, but addressed)

---

## §4. SOCIAL STABILITY LAYER

During crisis:

- Daily micro check-ins (2–5 min)
- Identify:
  - fear clusters
  - isolation
  - emerging “us vs them” patterns

Deploy:
- mediators
- rest rotations
- Refugium access prioritization

> Social collapse = system collapse

---

## §5. REVERSION

System exits Crisis Mode when:

- Baseline restored ≥95%  
- Resource flow stabilized  
- Psychological signals normalized  

> No fixed time window

---

### 5.1 Gradual Re-entry

- Step-down through levels (not instant reset)
- Maintain light monitoring for 7–14 days
- Prevent rebound collapse

---

## §6. ANTI-COERCION SAFEGUARDS

- No permanent authority created  
- All roles auto-expire  
- All decisions are reversible  

If coercion emerges:
→ system flags as failure condition

---

## §7. POST-CRISIS LEARNING

Mandatory:

- What failed first?  
- What held?  
- Where did humans struggle?  

Update:
- reserves
- thresholds
- protocols
