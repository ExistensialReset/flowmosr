# FLOW META-GOVERNANCE LAYER (FMGL)

**Status:** ACTIVE (OBSERVATIONAL LAYER)  
**Purpose:** Detect long-term systemic imbalance and adjust weighting of existing protocols  
**Principle:** The system does not decide better — it learns what it is becoming.

---

## §1. CORE FUNCTION

This layer does NOT:
- create new rules
- override decisions in real time
- replace CEPP, CRISIS, RESOURCE, or FCRL

This layer ONLY:
- observes outcomes over time
- detects recurring patterns
- suggests subtle rebalancing of system weights

---

## §2. OBSERVATION VARIABLES

The system continuously tracks:

### 2.1 Resource Distribution Balance
- Who consistently receives more access?
- Who consistently waits or loses priority?

### 2.2 Influence Balance
- Who tends to shape decisions through language, framing, or timing?
- Who is often overridden or ignored?

### 2.3 Exit Pressure (CEPP usage)
- Who frequently considers leaving?
- Is CEPP being used as a strategic tool or genuine exit?

### 2.4 Crisis Activation Bias
- Is CRISIS triggered too often or too rarely?
- Are thresholds aligned with real need?

### 2.5 Participation Stability
- Who stays engaged over time?
- Who disengages or becomes structurally marginal?

---

## §3. PATTERN DETECTION RULE

If a pattern persists across time:

- ≥3 cycles (or equivalent observation period)
- consistent directional imbalance
- not explained by crisis conditions

Then the system flags:

> “Systemic Drift Detected”

---

## §4. RESPONSE TYPE (IMPORTANT)

FMGL does NOT enforce changes directly.

Instead it produces:

### 4.1 Soft Rebalancing Suggestions
Examples:
- adjust weighting in RESOURCE simulation fairness
- increase or decrease sensitivity in CRISIS thresholds
- reduce strategic leverage of repeated CEPP invocation
- encourage rotation in decision influence roles

---

### 4.2 No Immediate Enforcement Rule

No adjustment is applied instantly.

All changes must:
- be reviewed in non-crisis conditions
- be discussed or acknowledged by Circles
- be reversible

---

## §5. META-PRINCIPLE

> The system must not optimize for single decisions,  
> but for long-term fairness of patterns.

---

## §6. SAFETY CONSTRAINT

FMGL must never:
- punish individual actors
- override CEPP rights
- create centralized authority
- interfere during active CRISIS mode

---

## §7. INTERPRETATION LAYER

FMGL is interpretive, not authoritative.

Final human context always overrides FMGL suggestions.

---

## §8. INTENT

This layer exists to answer one question:

> “Is the system slowly drifting into imbalance while still appearing fair in each individual decision?”

If yes → adjust gently  
If no → observe and do nothing

---

**End of Layer**
