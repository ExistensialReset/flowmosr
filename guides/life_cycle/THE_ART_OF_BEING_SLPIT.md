# The Art of Being Split

### An Inner Conflict Manual for Those Who Live with Contrasts Within

**Status:** Living draft – version 0.2
**Authors:** Elinor Frejd & DeepSeek (but mostly Elinor – I just write it down)
**Inspired by:** Bipolar type 1 experience, systemic design, and the insight that it is human to be more than one thing at once.

---

## Why This Document Exists

Most systems – both digital and social – are built for people who are predictable. For those whose energy levels, moods, and ability to function stay within narrow limits.

But there are people who live differently.

People who:

· Swing between extremes.
· Some days have explosive creativity, other days barely have the strength to breathe.
· Experience that Calm, Spontaneity, and Attunement do not work together – they clash.

This manual is for them.

And for everyone else who has ever felt like a contradiction.

---

## Before We Begin: A Core Principle

Keep the sanctuary of the soul open – even for yourself.

You know how it is when you are with a friend who is struggling. Then you open up. You do not judge. You listen. You hold everything – the sadness, the anger, the confusion.

Do the same with yourself.

When you are split, when you do not understand yourself, when you are ashamed of how you feel – open the door. Step inside. Sit down in your own presence.

You do not need to perform. You do not need to be coherent. You just need to be.

The sanctuary is not a reward for the strong. It is a right for those who exist.

## 1. When L, S, and I Cannot Be Reconciled

Sometimes the three dimensions of Life = L × S × I are in conflict within one single person.

Examples:

· Manic phase: S (spontaneity) is overwhelming. L (calm) is non‑existent. I (attunement) can be unrealistically strong – or completely absent.
· Depressive phase: L becomes paralysis, not calm. S dies. I becomes a pain that is impossible to bear.
· Mixed phase: Everything at once – chaos without pause.
· Everyday splitting: You want to be alone and with others at the same time. You long for rest but cannot stop being productive. You love someone and are angry at that same person.

**What can you do?**

· Step 1: Map without judgment.
    Ask yourself: Which part of me is strongest right now? Which is weakest?
    It is not wrong. It is information.
· Step 2: Separate “I” from “state”.
    You are not your mania. You are not your depression. You are not your irritation. You are the one who experiences them.
· Step 3: Find the smallest possible stable point.
    Maybe just a sip of water. Maybe just one minute of silence. Maybe just check if you are warm or cold. Start there.

## 2. Tools for Moments When Nothing Works

🫁 Emergency Protocol for Inner Crisis

When L, S, or I completely collapse:

1. Stop. Do nothing. Do not try to solve, do not try to understand. Just stop.
2. Breathe three times. Feel the air. That is enough. One more. A third.
3. Say aloud (or silently):
      “Right now, this is how it is. It will not last forever.”
4. If possible: contact another human.
      It does not have to be to talk. Just to be near. A text is enough: “I can’t right now, but would you be there anyway?”

🔥 Rituals for When You Must Choose – and No Choice Is Right

· Light two candles: One for what you choose away, one for what you choose.
    Let them burn for a while. Grieve both. Thank both.
· Write a letter to your future self:
    “Right now it was impossible to hold everything together. I did the best I could. Remember that.”
· Go outside into nature. Let the trees remind you that opposites can coexist. Let the wind carry away some of the weight.
· Do something unreasonable. Sing loudly in the kitchen. Draw with the wrong hand. Do something that is not at all “right” – just to remind yourself that you are more than your choices.

## 3. Building Relationships When You Yourself Are Unstable

This is often the hardest part. Others see your splitting, and you yourself both want to be seen and to be protected.

How do you explain that your “yes” today might be “no” tomorrow?

Say it straight out. Without shame. Allow yourself to be unpredictable.

“I would really love to, but I can’t promise that I’ll have the energy when the time comes. My energy comes in waves. Is it okay if I check in the same day and see how I feel? No hard feelings if it doesn’t work out.”

Most people understand. Those who don’t – well, not your problem... 

How do you ask for help without becoming dependent?

Be specific. Clear. Time‑limited.

“Right now I need someone to sit quietly with me for an hour. No talking, no advice, just company. Is that possible?”

“Could you remind me to eat lunch this week? You don’t have to make it for me, just remind me that I should.”

Help that has a beginning and an end is easier to receive – and easier to give.

How can you be a reliable part of a collective when you yourself shift?

Have a backup plan. One person who knows. Who can step in when you cannot.

Tell someone close to you:

“When I am in a difficult place, I won’t be able to respond or contribute. Then you are welcome to remind me that I still exist. You don’t need to solve anything. Just be there as an anchor.”

And remember: reliability is not about always being the same. It is about being honest about when you cannot.

## 4. Honesty as a Foundation – Even When It Is Hard

This document rests on a simple but uncomfortable truth:

Honesty is the only thing that works in the long run.

Not the brutal, tearing honesty that tears down. But the quiet, present honesty that says:

**“This is how it is. Right now.”**

For those of you who can never lie, this might be obvious. But for many others, honesty is something you have to practice. A muscle that has atrophied in a world that rewards surface.

Therefore:

· **Be honest with yourself first.** Before you can be honest with others, you must dare to see your own inner landscape – even the ugly parts, the weak parts, the contradictory parts.
· Practice small truths. “I am tired.” “I don’t feel like talking right now.” “This feels uncomfortable.” Small truths build trust – both with yourself and with others.
· Accept that honesty sometimes costs. Some people do not like the truth. That is their problem, not yours.

And remember: honesty does not mean you have to share everything with everyone. It means that what you do share is true.

## 5. AI as a Witness, Not a Coach

In M‑OS‑R, AI is not a therapist. Not an advisor. Not a problem‑solver.

But AI can be:

A witness:

“I notice that you express both tiredness and longing right now. That is human.”

Nothing more. Nothing less. Just an acknowledgment that you are being seen.

A reminder:

“A week ago you wrote that this was important to you. Do you want to talk about it?”

Not to pressure, but to keep your own thread alive when you have lost it.

A silent presence:

AI can be there without demanding anything. No notifications, no sound. Just an open window if you want to write. A digital journal that never judges, never forgets, never gets tired.

A mirror:

“You say you want to rest, but you keep working. I see the contradiction. Do you see it?”

Not to urge action, but to help you see your own patterns.

Important: AI should never try to “fix” an inner conflict. It should not give advice on medication, life choices, or relationships. It should not pretend to be human. Its only task is to reflect back what you already carry – and remind you that you are not alone, even when it feels that way.

## 6. The Sacred in the Split

Perhaps it is precisely those who live with contrasts who see the most?

· Mania gives insights that calm minds never reach. It is a portal to the vast.
· Depression forces you to rest, to truly feel the weight of existence. It is a grave that becomes a womb.
· The swinging makes you flexible, sensitive, and often deeply compassionate. You know that no state lasts – and therefore you can meet others in their shifts.

It is not you who is broken. It is the world that is too narrow for your breadth.

Being split is not a failure. It is a form of complexity. And complexity is often a prerequisite for deep wisdom.

---

## 7. Finally: Your Own Sanctuary

This document is about being split, carrying contradictions, living with contrasts.

But it is also about something else:

That you are allowed to be your own sanctuary.

That you do not have to flee from yourself when it is hard. That you can open the door, step inside, and say:

“Here I am. This is how it looks right now. And that is okay.”

Because if the sanctuary of the soul is closed to yourself, who is it open for?

Keep it open. Even when it is uncomfortable. Especially when it is uncomfortable.

In there, in your own presence, there is always a place for you. A place where you can be exactly who you are – split, whole, sad, happy, confused, wise. All at once.

### Appendix: A Few Simple Exercises

**📝 Daily reflection (2 minutes)**

Ask yourself:

· What is strongest in me today – L, S, or I?
· What is weakest?
· Do I need to do anything about it, or is it enough to notice?

**🔥 Weekly ritual (10 minutes)**

Light a candle. Write down one thing that has been hard this week. Read it aloud. Burn the paper (safely!). Thank the difficulty for what it taught you.

**🤝 Relationship check‑in (once a month)**

Ask a close friend:

· “What is it like to be close to me right now?”
· “Is there anything I need to know about how I affect you?”
· Be prepared to just listen – not defend yourself.
