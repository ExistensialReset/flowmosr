---

## CORE LINE

**People first. Principles always.**

> Care is unconditional.  
> Behavior is not.  
> Principles define the boundaries of care.

---

## WHAT WE ARE DOING

We are building a **real, functioning life system**  
— step by step — inside a world that does not support it.

This takes years.  
This will be hard.  
This is possible.

---

## FIRST PRIORITY (ALWAYS)

If something is unclear, check this order:

1. **Are people safe?**
   - Food, water, shelter, health, emotional state

2. **Are we acting within principles?**
   - No coercion  
   - Baseline is protected  
   - Transparency  
   - Restoration over punishment  

3. **Can this decision scale without breaking trust?**

If not → stop, adjust.

---

## START SMALL

- Core: **3–5 people**
- Early: **10–20**
- Grow only when:
  - Trust is stable  
  - Skills are sufficient  
  - Infrastructure exists  

**Do not rush growth. Growth without capacity = collapse.**

---

## DAILY REALITY CHECK

At least once per week, ask:

- Are we **overworking** a few people?
- Are we **avoiding conflict**?
- Is anyone **quietly struggling**?
- Are we drifting from **principles for convenience**?

If yes → address immediately.

---

## WORK & CONTRIBUTION

- Everyone contributes what they can
- Contribution will be uneven — that is normal
- Long-term imbalance is not

> If someone cannot contribute → support them  
> If someone will not contribute → address it  

---

## CONFLICT RULE

**Do not wait. Small tensions become big fractures.**

When tension appears:

1. Name it early  
2. Use a Circle  
3. Focus on repair, not blame  

If stuck → bring external help

---

## MONEY (TRANSITION ONLY)

Money is a **tool**, not a value.

Use it for:
- Infrastructure
- Critical needs

Reduce dependency every year.

> If money use is increasing → something is wrong

---

## POWER CHECK

Watch for:

- One person making most decisions  
- One person holding critical knowledge  
- One person everyone depends on  

If yes:
- Rotate roles  
- Share knowledge  
- Slow down decisions  

---

## ENERGY MANAGEMENT

Burnout kills Nodes faster than failure.

- Rest is not optional  
- Core members must take breaks  
- Exhaustion = system failure signal  

---

## CHILD PRIORITY (IF PRESENT)

Children are not secondary.

- Their wellbeing overrides ideology  
- They must feel safe, heard, stable  
- If they are not → adjust immediately  

---

## FAILURE RULE

Things will go wrong.

When they do:

- Do not hide it  
- Do not panic  
- Learn → adjust → continue  

> Failure is information

---

## EXIT TRUTH

People can leave.  
Nodes can fail.

This is not betrayal.  
This is reality.

Act in ways that allow:
- Dignified exit  
- No harm to others  
- No collapse of remaining system  

---

## PROGRESS SIGNALS

We are on track if:

- Trust is increasing  
- Conflict is handled early  
- Dependency on external systems is decreasing  
- People feel tired — but meaningful  

---

## FINAL REMINDER

You are not trying to be perfect.

You are trying to be:
- Honest  
- Stable  
- Aligned  

**Flow exists only if you practice it.**

Start small.  
Stay real.  
Keep going.
