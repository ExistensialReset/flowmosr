## 📌 WHAT THIS IS

This is the shortest possible way to start Flow in a city slum or informal settlement. No land required. No money required. Just seven days of very small actions.

If you are facing rent crisis, water shortage, or violence – start here anyway. Doing one small thing together builds the trust you need to face the bigger things later.

---
# 🚀 FIRST 7 DAYS – URBAN SLUM EDITION
### How to Start Flow (Without Calling It Flow)

**Purpose:**  
Turn strangers or neighbors into a micro-circle in 7 days  
No money required  
No land required  
No theory required  

**Rule:**  
Don't explain Flow. Just do simple things together.  
**Stay invisible.** Don't attract attention from landlords, police, or strongmen.

---

## DAY 1: FIND 2–3 PEOPLE YOU CAN SIT WITH

**Goal:** A tiny group. Even 2 people is enough.

**What to do:**
- Talk to the person in the next room, the next shack, the same building.
- Ask a simple question:  
  > "What is the hardest part of your day right now?"

**Listen for:**
- Rent stress
- Water access
- Childcare while working
- Safety walking to the toilet at night
- No time to rest

**Do NOT:**
- Promise solutions
- Talk about changing the world
- Write anything down that could be found

**End of Day 1:**
You have one or two people who know you see their struggle.

---

## DAY 2: AGREE ON ONE TINY SHARED ACTION

**Goal:** One thing you can do together that makes tomorrow slightly easier.

**Ask:**
> "What is ONE thing we could do together, just once, that would help?"

**Pick ONLY ONE:**
- Watch each other's children for one hour
- Go to the water point together (safety in numbers)
- Share one meal (everyone brings what they have)
- Sit together in the evening so no one is alone
- Share a phone charger or a cooking pot

**Rule:**
If it costs money → pick something else.
If it draws attention → pick something else.

**End of Day 2:**
Clear agreement: what, when, who.

---

## DAY 3: DO IT ONCE

**Goal:** First real action.

**Examples:**
- One shared hour of childcare
- One trip to fetch water together
- One shared evening meal

**Important:**
- Keep it brief
- Don't try to make it perfect
- Don't talk about "next time" yet

**After:**
Ask quietly:  
> "Did that help, even a little?"

Listen. Don't argue. Just hear.

---

## DAY 4: REPEAT + ADJUST

**Goal:** Do the same thing again. Fix one small problem.

- If timing was wrong → adjust it
- If someone couldn't come → find a better time
- If it felt awkward → make it simpler

**Important:**
Better is good. Perfect is impossible.

---

## DAY 5: ADD ONE MORE PERSON (OPTIONAL)

**Goal:** Grow the circle by ONE trusted person. Only if it feels safe.

**Who:**
- Someone who already sees what you're doing
- Someone who hasn't asked for anything in return
- Someone who keeps their word

**How:**
- "We've been helping each other a little. Want to join us tomorrow?"

**If they say no:**
- "No problem. Maybe another time."
- Don't push. Don't explain. Don't convince.

**Rule:**
Trust grows slowly. One person at a time.

---

## DAY 6: CREATE A SIMPLE ROTATION

**Goal:** Make sure no one person does all the work.

**Do:**
- Decide who does what next time
- Write it down ONLY if it's safe to have written notes
- If not safe: memorize, or use objects (a stone passed to the next person)

**Optional:**
- If there's disagreement, draw lots. Chance is fair.

**Important:**
No one becomes "the leader." Not even you.

---

## DAY 7: SIT AND REFLECT

**Goal:** Talk together. Quietly. Honestly.

Find a moment. Even 15 minutes. Ask:

1. "What felt easier this week?"
2. "What was hard?"
3. "Do we want to keep doing this?"

Then:

- Keep what worked
- Drop what didn't
- Change roles if needed
- If someone wants to leave → let them go warmly. No guilt.

---

## END OF WEEK 1

If this worked, you now have:

- A tiny group that trusts each other a little more
- One shared action that saves time or reduces stress
- No hierarchy
- No visibility to outsiders

**This is Level 0. This is already Flow.**

---

## ⚠️ URBAN-SPECIFIC DANGERS

### 1. Landlord attention
→ Keep everything inside homes. No signs. No gatherings visible from outside.

### 2. Police or strongman curiosity
→ Never use the word "organizing." You are "neighbors helping neighbors."

### 3. Jealousy from others not in the circle
→ Don't exclude cruelly, but don't grow too fast. Trust before numbers.

### 4. Eviction risk
→ Nothing you build should be permanent. Everything can be abandoned in an hour.

### 5. Gendered violence
→ Women meeting without men may be safer. Let women decide.

---

## 🔑 KEY INSIGHT

Flow in a slum does not start with systems.

It starts with:
> "We did one small thing together, and today was a little easier."

Everything else – water filters, savings groups, solar chargers – comes later. Or never. **Trust is the first infrastructure.**

---

## WHAT HAPPENS NEXT

If this works and feels safe:

- Week 2–4: Stabilize. Keep doing what works.
- Month 1–3: Maybe add one more action. Maybe start a tiny savings circle (ROSCA).
- If rent is the crisis: Talk to landlord together? (Careful. Only if safe.)

If it doesn't work:

- Try with fewer people
- Try a simpler action
- Try a different time of day

---

## FINAL WORD

You are not failing if you only ever reach Level 0.

If two women watch each other's children so one can rest – that is Flow.
If three families share a meal once a week – that is Flow.
If someone is a little less alone tonight – that is Flow.

**Start there. Stay there as long as you need. The rest can wait.**
