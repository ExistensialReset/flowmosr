## PURPOSE

> "Expertise is real. Expert privilege is dangerous. We need the first without creating the second."

**The Challenge:**

LOTUS uses expertise weighting for technical decisions:
- Doctors vote more on healthcare
- Engineers vote more on infrastructure
- Ecologists vote more on environmental policy

**But this creates capture risk:**
- Who decides who is an expert?
- Who certifies them?
- How do we prevent expert cartels?
- How do we stop technocracy creep?

**This protocol specifies HOW expertise is certified without creating a new elite class.**

---

## CORE PROBLEM

**Expertise weighting is necessary** (we don't want non-engineers designing bridges)

**But expertise certification is a power point** (who certifies has power)

**Tension:**
- Need to recognize real competence
- Must prevent expert capture of governance
- Must allow expertise to evolve/rotate
- Must keep it democratic

---

## SOLUTION: DISTRIBUTED, TEMPORARY, TRANSPARENT CERTIFICATION

**Five anti-capture mechanisms:**

1. **Peer Certification** (experts don't self-certify, peers do)
2. **Time Limits** (expertise weight decays over time)
3. **Transparency** (all certifications public, auditable)
4. **Multiple Pathways** (formal education OR practical experience OR peer recognition)
5. **LOTUS Override** (community can challenge any certification)

---

## MECHANISM 1: Peer Certification Process

**Core Principle:** Experts are certified by OTHER experts in their field, not by institutions or themselves.

### How It Works:

**Step 1: Self-Nomination**
- Person claims expertise in domain (e.g., "I am a competent electrician")
- Submits evidence:
  - Training/education (formal or informal)
  - Practical experience (projects completed, hours worked)
  - Peer testimonials (optional)

**Step 2: Peer Review Panel**
- 3-5 already-certified experts in same domain randomly selected
- Review evidence
- Interview candidate (technical questions, practical demonstration)
- Vote: Certify or Not

**Step 3: Certification**
- If ≥60% of panel approves → Certified
- If <60% → Not certified (can reapply after 6 months)
- Certification level assigned: Novice, Competent, Expert, Master

**Step 4: Registration**
- Certification registered in Flow-ID
- Public record (anyone can verify)
- Effective immediately

### Example Certification Process:

```
ELECTRICIAN CERTIFICATION - Candidate: Alex
─────────────────────────────────────────────
​SELF-NOMINATION:
├─ Training: 2-year apprenticeship under Master Electrician Sara
├─ Experience: 500 hours electrical work across 30 projects
├─ Projects: Wired 5 buildings, repaired 40+ electrical systems
└─ Testimonials: 3 peer testimonials (Sara, Mika, Jordan)
​PEER REVIEW PANEL (randomly selected):
├─ Reviewer 1: Master Electrician Sara
├─ Reviewer 2: Expert Electrician Mika
├─ Reviewer 3: Competent Electrician Jordan
​REVIEW PROCESS:
├─ Technical Interview: 45 minutes
│   └─ Questions on circuit design, safety, troubleshooting
├─ Practical Demonstration: 2 hours
│   └─ Wire control panel, diagnose fault, design simple circuit
└─ Portfolio Review: Sara verified project quality
​PANEL VOTES:
├─ Sara: CERTIFY (Competent level)
├─ Mika: CERTIFY (Competent level)
└─ Jordan: CERTIFY (Novice level - needs more complex experience)
​RESULT: CERTIFIED - Competent Electrician
├─ Effective: Immediately
├─ LOTUS Weight: 2.0× on electrical infrastructure decisions
├─ Expires: 5 years (renewable)
└─ Public Record: Yes, auditable via Flow-ID
```

## MECHANISM 2: Certification Levels & Weighting

**Not all expertise is equal. We use tiered system:**

| Level | Criteria | LOTUS Weight | Can Certify Others? |
|-------|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Novice** | Basic training, <200 hours experience | 1.2× | No |
| **Competent** | Formal training OR 500+ hours experience | 2.0× | Yes (Novice only) |
| **Expert** | Advanced training AND 2,000+ hours | 2.5× | Yes (up to Competent) |
| **Master** | 10+ years, recognized innovation/teaching | 3.0× | Yes (up to Expert) |

**Weight Decay:**
- After 5 years: Weight reduces by 0.5× annually
- At 10 years: Must recertify to maintain weight
- **Why:** Knowledge becomes outdated, prevents permanent expert class

## MECHANISM 3: Multiple Pathways to Expertise

**Formal education is NOT required.** Expertise can be proven through:

### Pathway 1: Formal Education
- Degree/certification from recognized institution
- Still requires peer review (degree ≠ automatic certification)

### Pathway 2: Apprenticeship/Practical Experience
- Work under certified expert
- Document hours and projects

### Pathway 3: Self-Taught + Demonstration
- Extensive practical experience
- Demonstrate competence through portfolio + technical interview

### Pathway 4: Innovation/Research
- Created new technique, tool, or knowledge
- Recognized contribution to field

## MECHANISM 4: Domain Mapping & Specialization

**Major Domains:**
- **Healthcare:** Medicine, Surgery, Mental Health, Midwifery, etc.
- **Engineering:** Electrical, Structural, Software, Agricultural, etc.
- **Ecology/Environment:** Ecosystem Management, Climate Science, Soil Science.
- **Education:** Pedagogy, Curriculum Design, Special Education.
- **Governance/Law:** Conflict Mediation, Policy Analysis.
- **Arts/Craft:** Architecture, Carpentry, Metalwork, Culinary.

---

## MECHANISM 5: Anti-Capture Safeguards

### Safeguard 1: Distributed Certification (No Central Authority)
Each domain self-regulates via distributed, randomly selected peer panels.

### Safeguard 2: Public Transparency
All certifications, panel members, and evidence reviewed are public record.

### Safeguard 3: Community Challenge
Any certification can be challenged and investigated via LOTUS.

### Safeguard 4: Expertise Weight Caps
Maximum weight is capped at 3.0× (Master level) to ensure non-experts still have a voice.

### Safeguard 5: Domain Rotation (Cultural)
Encourage people to develop expertise in multiple domains over their lifetime.

---

## FAILURE MODES & RESPONSES

1. **Expert Cartel Formation:** Enforce random panel selection and transparency audits.
2. **Credential Inflation:** Standard reviews and weight adjustments if necessary.
3. **Gatekeeping:** Appeals process and bias training for panelists.
4. **Technocracy Creep:** Weight caps and plain-language requirements for proposals.
5. **Stale Expertise:** Mandatory recertification and weight decay.
