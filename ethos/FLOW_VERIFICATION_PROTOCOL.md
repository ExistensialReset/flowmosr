# FLOW_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL.md

**Status:** ACTIVE RESOURCE INTEGRITY  
**Purpose:** Ensure accurate, accountable monitoring and allocation of resources across Nodes and Regions  
**Principle:** Flow accuracy protects Baseline; all corrective actions must be verified and proportionate.

---

## PREAMBLE

Flow discrepancies may appear as:  
- Missing food, water, or energy units  
- Misrouted resources between Nodes  
- Unexpected losses in supply chains  
- Conflicting reports from Nodes  

Unchecked, discrepancies can threaten Baseline stability. All responses must be **verified by named human decision-makers** and documented.

---

## §1. TRIGGER CONDITIONS

Protocol triggers **advisory flags** when:  
- L×S×I data indicates potential scarcity  
- Resource accounting shows discrepancies exceeding tolerance thresholds  
- Reports of failed deliveries, unexplained shortages, or hoarding  

**Important:** All triggers are **advisory only**. Any redistribution or corrective action **requires human verification** before execution.  

**Baseline Emergency Advisory:**  
- If immediate threat to Baseline exists (food, water, medical, shelter), the system may **flag for expedited human review**.  
- Temporary resource allocation may be proposed but **cannot bypass human authorization**.  
- Named human decision-makers must document rationale, scope, and duration.

---

## §2. DETECTION & TRIAGE

### 2.1 Automated Monitoring (Advisory Only)
- AI cross-checks inventory and consumption reports  
- Flags anomalies exceeding tolerance thresholds  
- Logs discrepancies for human review  
- AI does **not** execute corrections without human authorization

### 2.2 Human Observation
- Regional coordinators verify flagged discrepancies  
- Investigators check transport logs, storage, and consumption  
- Document all observations, rationale, and decisions

### 2.3 AI-Assisted Triage
- Minor discrepancies (<10% Baseline impact): AI review + 1–2 humans  
- Medium discrepancies (10–50% Baseline impact): AI + 2–3 humans  
- Major discrepancies (>50% Baseline impact): Full team (5–7 humans) + external oversight  
- **All triage recommendations require named human approval before action**

---

## §3. INVESTIGATION

- Identify missing, misallocated, or delayed resources  
- Map flows between Nodes, Regions, and sub-units  
- Determine cause: human error, sabotage, AI miscalculation, or external event  
- Assess L×S×I impact and potential Baseline risk  
- Document all findings and decision points

---

## §4. RESPONSE PATHWAYS

### 4.1 Correction
- Reallocate resources **only after human verification**  
- Adjust accounting to reflect verified availability  
- Verify delivery and consumption through independent observation

### 4.2 Containment
- Temporarily isolate Nodes or channels causing repeated discrepancies  
- Limit further transfers until verification is complete  
- Maintain Baseline security at all times

### 4.3 Escalation
- Suspected sabotage → escalate to **INFRASTRUCTURE_SABOTAGE_PROTOCOL**  
- Divergence in AI calculations → escalate to **AI_DIVERGENCE_PROTOCOL**  
- Immediate Baseline threat → activate **BASELINE_EMERGENCY_BYPASS** (human-verified)

---

## §5. COMPETENCE REQUIREMENTS

- At least 1 resource logistics expert per team  
- Access to regional inventory and AI monitoring systems  
- Training in discrepancy investigation and human oversight  

If competence gaps exist:  
- Request regional support  
- Document knowledge gaps and mitigation plans  
- Avoid unilateral action without verification

---

## §6. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER PROTOCOLS

- **INFRASTRUCTURE_SABOTAGE_PROTOCOL:** for intentional interference  
- **AI_DIVERGENCE_PROTOCOL:** for AI misalignment affecting flow  
- **CRISIS_PROTOCOL:** for immediate life-threatening shortages  
- **PROTOCOL_PRIORITY_MATRIX:** to determine execution order when multiple protocols are triggered
