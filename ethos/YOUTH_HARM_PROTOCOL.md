# YOUTH_HARM_PROTOCOL.md

**Status:** ACTIVE CHILD PROTECTION  
**Purpose:** Prevent and respond to harm affecting minors in Nodes  
**Principle:** Youth Baseline is inviolable; early detection saves lives.

---

## PREAMBLE

Minors are inherently vulnerable. Harm may manifest as:  
- Physical abuse  
- Emotional neglect  
- Exploitation  
- Exposure to dangerous resources or environments  

**Both slow, hidden harm and acute incidents are critical.**  

All protective interventions are **human-verified**; AI may assist as advisory only.

---

## §1. TRIGGER CONDITIONS

Protocol activates when:  
- Reports of harm or neglect from any Node member  
- Sudden or sustained drop in L×S×I among minors  
- Patterns of absenteeism, isolation, or fear  

**Baseline Emergency Advisory:**  
- Expedited human assessment is required before activating housing, care, or emergency support.  
- Any protective action is **documented** and assigned to a **named human decision-maker**.  
- Time guidelines (e.g., 24 hours) are advisory and operational targets, **not automatic triggers**.

---

## §2. DETECTION & TRIAGE

### 2.1 Observation
- Teachers, caregivers, and peers report unusual behaviors  
- AI-assisted monitoring may flag participation and L×S×I trends **for human review only**  

### 2.2 Rapid Assessment
- Trauma-informed screening by trained humans  
- Psychological safety evaluation of minor and involved staff  
- Assign advocate/support verified by a named human decision-maker  

### 2.3 AI-Assisted Triage (Advisory)
- **Minor risk:** AI + 1 trained observer advisory review  
- **Medium risk:** AI + 2–3 trained humans, decisions confirmed by named human verifier  
- **High risk:** Full child protection team + external oversight; AI informs prioritization, but human verification required for all interventions  

---

## §3. INTERVENTION PATHWAYS

### 3.1 Immediate Safety (Verified)
- Remove minor from harmful environment **only with human verification**  
- Provide safe housing, food, healthcare under documented human oversight  
- Notify regional child protection coordination; all steps logged and assigned a named human decision-maker  

### 3.2 Investigation
- Determine source, duration, and type of harm  
- Map Node structures, caregivers, and exposure pathways  
- Conduct psychological evaluation **under human supervision**  
- All findings are documented for accountability  

### 3.3 Remediation
- Counseling and trauma support **assigned and supervised by humans**  
- Repair relationships and Node environment  
- Education and reintegration plans are reviewed and verified by named decision-makers  

### 3.4 Escalation
- If criminal activity: coordinate with **LEGAL_INTERFACE_PROTOCOL**, verified by named human decision-maker  
- If systemic Node failure: consider **SLOW_EROSION_PROTOCOL** or **REGIONAL_DEADLOCK_PROTOCOL**, human-approved  
- AI recommendations may inform prioritization but cannot override human verification  

---

## §4. COMPETENCE REQUIREMENTS

- Child protection certification  
- Trauma-informed care knowledge  
- Ability to coordinate with AI monitoring advisory outputs  
- Access to regional support and escalation pathways  

All interventions **must be executed by competent, human-verified personnel**.

---

## §5. METRICS & FEEDBACK LOOP

Document and record:  
- L×S×I improvement for minor  
- Node environment remediation  
- Intervention speed and effectiveness  

Feed all data into regional and global child safety metrics **with human verification**.

---

## §6. PHILOSOPHICAL GROUND

- Youth Baseline is inviolable  
- Prevention is prioritized over remediation  
- Early detection saves lives and trust  
- AI is advisory; humans make verified, accountable decisions  

**Final Truth:** Protecting minors preserves Node and Flow integrity.

---

## CLOSING PRINCIPLE

All actions are **expedited, trauma-informed, human-verified, and fully documented**, prioritizing the minor’s Baseline and autonomy.
