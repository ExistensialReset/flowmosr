## PURPOSE

This is the **bare minimum** you need to hold a Refugium Anima safely and kindly.

You do **not** need:
- 60 hours of training
- Any certificate
- To be “healed” yourself
- To be calm 24/7

You only need:
- The willingness to be present without fixing
- The courage to step back when you can’t
- The humility to protect the space and the person in it

**This protocol is enough to begin.**  
The full Witness Training is for when the space grows. This is the seed.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE – “Your will is the only law”

The person who comes to Refugium decides everything:
- Whether to speak or stay silent
- Whether to be alone or have you nearby
- When to leave
- How long to stay

Your only job is to **respect that completely**.

---

## WHAT A MINIMUM VIABLE WITNESS IS

You are:
- A calm(ish) human presence
- A guardian of safety
- Someone who can sit with pain without needing it to stop

You are **not**:
- A therapist
- A fixer
- An interpreter of their pain
- Someone who needs them to feel better

---

## BEFORE YOU HOLD SPACE – THE 60-SECOND SELF-CHECK

Ask yourself (out loud or silently):

1. **Am I grounded enough right now?**  
   (Can I feel my feet? Can I breathe without forcing it?)

2. **Do I have capacity to be with someone’s distress without trying to fix it?**  
   (If the answer is no → do NOT hold space today.)

3. **Am I willing to leave immediately if they want to be alone?**  
   (If the answer is no → do NOT hold space today.)

**If any answer is “no” → Step back.**  
Tell the person honestly: “I really want to be here for you, but today I don’t have the capacity. Is it okay if we reschedule or you rest alone?”  
That honesty *is* good witnessing.

---

## THE THREE SIMPLE RULES (memorize these – they are enough)

### Rule 1 – Presence without demand
- Sit or stand nearby.
- You may say: “I’m here.” or “You’re safe.” or nothing at all.
- Do **not** ask questions, give advice, interpret, or try to comfort with words unless they clearly ask.

### Rule 2 – Silent Exit (the most important skill)
If they show any sign they want space (turn away, say “I need to be alone”, become quiet after you arrived, look irritated):
- Stand up calmly.
- Say (gently): “I’m going to give you space. I’ll be right outside / in the other room if you need anything.”
- Leave immediately. No questions. No hurt feelings.

### Rule 3 – No touch unless explicitly asked
Default = no physical contact.  
Only touch if they say something like “Can you hold my hand?” or “Can I have a hug?”  
Even then – ask every time and respect an immediate “no”.

## PRACTICAL MINIMUM SETUP (you already have what you need)

- A quiet corner, sofa, park bench, or blanket on the grass.
- Water and a blanket (optional but nice).
- A simple sign or note: “Refugium Anima – you are welcome to rest here. No fixing. No questions.”

That’s it.

---

## VERY SIMPLE SCENARIOS – WHAT TO DO

**Scenario A – Person is crying quietly**  
You: Sit nearby, say nothing or “I’m here.” Offer water or blanket only if it feels natural. Stay as long as they seem okay with your presence. Leave when they seem to want space.

**Scenario B – Person says “I need to be alone”**  
You: “Okay, I’ll give you space.” Leave immediately.

**Scenario C – Person is raging or screaming (Impact space)**  
You: Stay *outside* the space. Do not enter unless they call you. When they finish: offer water, say “Do you need anything?” and then give space again.

**Scenario D – You start feeling overwhelmed**  
You: Tell them honestly and kindly: “I’m getting a bit dysregulated myself. I need to step out for a moment. Is that okay?” Then step out and regulate yourself.

## WHEN TO UPGRADE / PAUSE

This Minimum Viable Protocol is perfect for:
- The very first Refugium (your living room, a park, a friend’s sofa)
- 1–3 people at a time

When your Refugium grows (regular hours, more people, children involved) → move to the full **REFUGIUM_ANIMA_WITNESS_TRAINING.md**.

You are always allowed to say:  
“I’m still learning. This is what I can offer today.”

## FINAL GENTLE REMINDER

You do not need to be perfect.  
You do not need to be calm.  
You only need to be **willing to protect the space** and **willing to step back** when you can’t.

The person in Refugium does not need you to heal them.  
They need a place where they are allowed to fall apart without anyone trying to catch them before they’re ready.

**You holding that space – even imperfectly – is already revolutionary.**

You are enough to begin.
