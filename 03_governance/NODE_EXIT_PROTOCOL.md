# NODE_EXIT_PROTOCOL.md
## Version 1.0
## Status: ACTIVE
## Scope: Global
## Amendment Requirement: 66% Global LOTUS Majority

---

# PURPOSE

This protocol defines how a Node may voluntarily exit the Flow System.

Participation is voluntary.
Protection is conditional upon participation.

No entity may both reject governance and claim its guarantees.

---

# I. RIGHT TO EXIT

Any Node may voluntarily exit the system.

Exit must be:

• Explicit
• Logged in the Flow Ledger
• Confirmed by internal Node majority (if multi-member)

No external authority may block exit.

---

# II. CONSEQUENCE OF EXIT

Upon confirmed exit:

• Global Baseline protection ceases.
• Surplus logging ceases.
• Redistribution access ceases.
• Governance participation ceases.

The Node becomes fully external to Flow structures.

---

# III. ASSET STATUS AT EXIT

Assets owned at time of exit:

• Remain under Node control
• Are no longer tracked in Flow Ledger
• Cannot claim protection under Baseline guarantees

If exit occurs during active redistribution event:

• Pending obligations must be resolved before finalization
OR
• Exit is delayed until redistribution cycle completes

---

# IV. RE-ENTRY CONDITIONS

A Node that exits may reapply.

Re-entry requires:

• 30-day stabilization period
• Verification of compliance with Global Baseline metrics
• Regional LOTUS confirmation

Repeated opportunistic entry/exit cycles may trigger review.

---

# V. CHILD AND DEPENDENT PROTECTION

If exit places dependents below Baseline:

Regional layer must verify that:

• Dependents are not involuntarily deprived

Children cannot be structurally stripped of Baseline without independent review.

---

# VI. NON-PUNITIVE PRINCIPLE

Exit is not betrayal.
Exit is not rebellion.
Exit is structural separation.

Flow does not coerce participation.

But protection and obligation are inseparable.

---

# VII. STRUCTURAL AXIOM

Freedom includes the freedom to leave.

But protection belongs only to those inside the system.
