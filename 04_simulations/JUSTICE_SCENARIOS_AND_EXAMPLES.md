## SCENARIO 1: NOISE CONFLICT (LAYER 1 → LAYER 2)

### THE SITUATION

**Parties:**
- Alex (plays music late at night, Soul Dwelling 12)
- Sam (neighbor below, Soul Dwelling 11)

**Complaint:**
Sam reports Alex plays bass-heavy music after 23:00, violating acoustic norms.

**Initial classification:** Level 1 (Social Tension)

---

### LAYER 1 ATTEMPT (FAILED)

**What happened:**
- Alex and Sam spoke directly
- Alex apologized, said they'd use headphones
- Music continued
- Sam feels dismissed

**Why it failed:**
Alex didn't understand impact. Sam didn't escalate clearly.

**Result:** Escalate to Layer 2

---

### LAYER 2 SESSION

**Facilitator:** Maya

**Opening:**
Maya sets ground rules. Both agree to participate.

**Sam's sharing (20 min):**
"I hear bass through the floor after 23:00 most nights. I use earplugs, but it still wakes me. I have early work. I feel like my need for sleep doesn't matter to Alex. When I asked them to stop, they said sorry but nothing changed. I feel helpless."

**Alex's sharing (20 min):**
"Music is how I regulate. After stressful days, I need to listen loud to feel present. I did apologize and I tried headphones, but they don't work for me – I need to feel the bass physically. I didn't realize it was still bothering Sam because they didn't say anything after our first talk."

**Reflection (Maya):**
"Sam, I hear that your sleep is being disrupted and you feel unheard. Alex, I hear that music is your regulation tool and headphones don't meet that need. It seems the first conversation didn't establish ongoing communication."

**Harm articulation:**
- **Sam's harm:** Sleep loss, feeling powerless, health impact
- **Alex's need:** Sensory regulation through music
- **System gap:** No clear escalation path after Layer 1

**Agreement:**

1. **Immediate:** Alex stops bass-heavy music after 23:00
2. **Alternative regulation:** Alex explores tactile alternatives (weighted blanket, subwoofer with isolated platform, scheduled loud music 20:00-22:30)
3. **Communication:** If issue persists, Sam messages Alex next day (not in the moment)
4. **Check-in:** 2-week follow-up with Maya

**Signed:** Both parties

**Perceived Fairness Protocol:**
- Sam: "Yes, fair."
- Alex: "Yes, I feel heard and I can work with this."

**Outcome:** Case closed. Follow-up scheduled.

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- Facilitator didn't take sides
- Both needs named
- Solution addresses both (quiet for Sam, regulation for Alex)
- Clear timeline and accountability

## SCENARIO 2: CALM DOMINANCE (LAYER 2 → SMD ACTIVATED)

### THE SITUATION

**Parties:**
- Jordan (accused of subtle controlling behavior)
- Casey (feels manipulated in shared projects)

**Complaint:**
Casey reports Jordan consistently reframes decisions, dismisses concerns as "anxiety," and positions themselves as the "stable" one.

**Initial classification:** Level 2 (Active Harm)

---

### LAYER 2 SESSION

**Facilitator:** Dani  
**Human Mirror:** River

**Casey's sharing:**
"I bring ideas to our projects and Jordan says 'That's interesting, but what you're really trying to say is...' and then rephrases it differently. When I push back, they say 'I'm just trying to help you communicate clearly.' I feel like I can't think for myself around them. When I said I felt uncomfortable with a decision, Jordan said 'That's your anxiety talking, not the real issue.' I feel crazy."

**Jordan's sharing:**
"I care about Casey. I see them struggle with articulation sometimes and I genuinely want to help. I have more experience with these kinds of projects, so I'm offering guidance. When Casey gets anxious, I try to ground them by separating emotion from fact. I think they're interpreting support as control, which isn't my intention."

---

### SMD (SUBTLE MANIPULATION DETECTION) ACTIVATED

**River (Human Mirror) observes:**
- Jordan has spoken for 65% of time despite facilitator's time allocation
- Jordan uses "Casey struggles with X" framing repeatedly
- Jordan reframes Casey's concerns as emotional dysregulation
- Jordan maintains calm, reasonable tone throughout

**River names it:**
"I want to name a pattern I'm seeing. Jordan, you've used phrases like 'Casey struggles,' 'what Casey really means,' and 'that's anxiety talking' multiple times. This positions you as interpreter of Casey's reality. Casey, you've become quieter as the session progressed. I'm wondering if this dynamic is happening here, now."

**Dani (Facilitator) responds:**
"Thank you, River. Jordan, I'm going to ask you to speak only about your own experience from this point. Not your interpretation of Casey. Casey, I'm giving you space now – what's your direct experience?"

---

### CORRECTED SESSION

**Casey (with redistributed space):**
"When Jordan says I 'struggle,' it makes me sound incompetent. I don't struggle – I have a different perspective. I feel like I'm being managed, not collaborated with."

**Jordan (redirected):**
"I... I didn't realize I was doing that. I thought I was being helpful."

**Dani:**
"Jordan, impact matters more than intent here. Casey is naming how this lands."

---

### AGREEMENT

1. **Immediate:** Jordan stops reframing Casey's words
2. **Practice:** Jordan asks clarifying questions instead of interpreting ("Can you say more about that?" vs. "What you mean is...")
3. **Accountability:** If Casey feels reframed, they say "That's not what I said" and Jordan restates
4. **Check-in:** 1 month follow-up

**Perceived Fairness Protocol:**
- Casey: "Yes, it felt fair after River named the pattern."
- Jordan: "I feel defensive, but I understand the feedback."

**Outcome:** Case closed with awareness Jordan may need deeper reflection.

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- Human Mirror caught pattern facilitator missed initially
- Facilitator adjusted mid-session
- Pattern named neutrally, not accusatorially
- Agreement is behavioral and trackable

## SCENARIO 3: REPEATED HARM ESCALATION (LAYER 2 → LAYER 3)

### THE SITUATION

**Parties:**
- Morgan (multiple conflict reports over 6 months)
- Various community members

**Complaint:**
Pattern of conflicts where Morgan becomes verbally aggressive when challenged.

**Initial classification:** Level 3 (Repeated Harm)

**RHE (Repeated Harm Escalation) activated:** 3 similar incidents / 6 months

---

### LAYER 3 PROCESS (LOTUS PANEL)

**Panel:** 5 people (cross-cluster), external observer

**Process:**

1. **Case presentation:** 3 previous incidents reviewed
2. **Morgan's perspective:** "I feel attacked when people question my work. I react defensively. I don't mean to hurt people."
3. **Pattern analysis:** When criticized, Morgan raises voice, uses accusatory language, dismisses others' concerns
4. **Safety assessment:** No physical threats, but verbal intensity creates fear

---

### PANEL DELIBERATION

**Question:** Is this harm from dysregulation or willful aggression?

**Assessment:**
- Morgan appears to struggle with emotional regulation under stress
- Pattern is consistent (trigger → escalation)
- Morgan expresses remorse after, but behavior repeats

**Decision:** This is a regulation issue, not malice. Requires support + boundaries.

---

### OUTCOME

**Structural actions:**

1. **Morgan attends emotional regulation support** (Refugium Anima access + weekly check-in)
2. **Work participation adjusted:** Morgan removed from high-stress coordination roles temporarily
3. **Communication boundary:** If Morgan escalates in meetings, meeting pauses immediately
4. **3-month review:** Assess if pattern has shifted

**Not punishment.** This is safety + support.

**Morgan's response:**
"I don't like this, but I understand. I need help with this."

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- Pattern recognized as systemic, not one-off
- Response balances support (Refugium) with protection (boundaries)
- Clear timeline for review
- Baseline maintained (Morgan still housed, fed, included in community)

## SCENARIO 4: CASE THROTTLING PROTOCOL (CTP ACTIVATED)

### THE SITUATION

**Party:**
- Taylor (submits 8 reports in 2 months)

**Reports against:**
Various people for: tone in meetings, not responding quickly enough, "hostile energy"

**Pattern Context Layer (PCL) signals:**
- High report frequency from Taylor
- Low external corroboration (others don't see issues)
- Distributed targets (not one ongoing conflict)

**CTP (Case Throttling Protocol) activated**

---

### TRIAGE ASSESSMENT

**Questions:**
1. Is Taylor experiencing genuine repeated harm?
2. Is Taylor using reporting as control mechanism?
3. Is there a mental health component?

**Assessment:**
- Most reports are about interpersonal friction (normal)
- Taylor's threshold for "harm" is unusually low
- Taylor appears to be in heightened anxiety state

---

### RESPONSE

**Actions:**

1. **Cases batched:** Not processed individually but reviewed as pattern
2. **Direct conversation:** Facilitator meets with Taylor one-on-one
3. **Mental health support offered:** "It seems like you're experiencing a lot of friction. Would Refugium Anima or other support help?"
4. **Reporting guidelines clarified:** "Reports are for safety issues and clear harm, not general interpersonal discomfort"

**Result:**
Taylor accesses Refugium Anima. Reports decrease. Taylor later shares they were experiencing burnout and everything felt threatening.

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- System didn't punish Taylor
- Pattern recognized as distress signal, not manipulation
- Baseline maintained (access to reporting)
- Bandwidth protected (facilitators not overwhelmed)

## SCENARIO 5: JUSTICE LIGHT MODE (JLM ACTIVATED)

### THE SITUATION

**Context:**
- 6 active cases simultaneously
- 2 facilitators burned out
- Community participation dropping

**JLM activated:** System overwhelmed

---

### MODE SHIFT

**Paused:**
- All Level 1 cases (referred to direct conversation)
- Non-urgent Level 2 cases (waitlisted)

**Prioritized:**
- Safety-critical cases only
- Severe harm cases

**Simplified:**
- Session length reduced (90 min → 60 min)
- Fewer participants in panels
- Documentation streamlined

---

### COMMUNICATION

**To community:**
"We're experiencing high case volume. We're prioritizing safety-critical cases. Other cases will be handled as capacity returns. This is temporary. We're protecting the system's sustainability."

---

### RECOVERY

**Actions:**

1. **Recruit 2 new facilitators** (accelerated training)
2. **Reduce meeting frequency** (weekly → biweekly)
3. **Mandatory rest for burned-out facilitators**

**Timeline:** 6 weeks to return to normal capacity

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- System degraded gracefully instead of collapsing
- Transparency about limitations
- Prioritization clear (safety first)
- Recovery plan activated

## SCENARIO 6: CHILD WITNESS ACTIVATED

### THE SITUATION

**Parties:**
- Parent A and Parent B (co-parenting conflict)
- Child (age 8)

**Complaint:**
Parents have unresolved conflict affecting child's sense of safety

**Classification:** Level 2, **Child Witness required**

---

### PROCESS

**Child Witness Specialist:** Kris (120h training)

**Before session:**
- Kris meets with child separately (play-based assessment)
- Child shares: "I feel scared when they yell. I don't know where I'm supposed to be."

**During session:**
- Kris represents child's perspective without child present
- "The child experiences this conflict as unpredictability and fear. They need to know where they'll sleep and that both parents are safe."

**Agreement:**

1. **Immediate:** No conflict discussions in child's presence
2. **Stability:** Fixed schedule communicated to child 1 week ahead
3. **Repair:** Both parents reassure child they are safe and loved
4. **Follow-up:** 2-week check-in with child (via Kris)

---

### WHAT WENT WELL

- Child's voice included without exposing them to process
- Specialist translated child's experience
- Agreement centered child's needs

## LESSONS ACROSS SCENARIOS

### GOOD FACILITATION LOOKS LIKE:

- Structure held even when emotions high
- Both parties given equal space
- Patterns named neutrally
- Solutions address needs, not blame
- Self-care and limits respected

### POOR FACILITATION LOOKS LIKE:

- Facilitator takes sides
- Structure abandoned under pressure
- Manipulation unnoticed
- Agreements vague or unenforceable
- Facilitator pushes through own exhaustion

### SYSTEM HEALTH INDICATORS:

- Perceived Fairness Protocol yields mostly "yes"
- Load is distributed (not one facilitator doing everything)
- Escalation happens when needed (not avoided)
- Light Mode activates before collapse
