## PURPOSE

Witnesses in Refugium Anima are **anchors, not therapists**.

You are not here to:
- Fix anyone
- Interpret anyone's pain
- Guide anyone toward "healing"
- Measure anyone's progress

You are here to:
- Hold the space
- Be present without demand
- Witness collapse without judgment
- Protect sanctuary from intrusion

**This is sacred work. It requires training, boundaries, and deep humility.**

---

## I. CORE PHILOSOPHY

### THE WITNESS PRINCIPLE

**"Your will is the only law."**

The person in Refugium decides:
- Whether to speak or be silent
- Whether to be alone or accompanied
- Whether to scream, weep, create, or sleep
- When to leave

**Your role:**
Respect that sovereignty completely.

---

### WHAT WITNESSING IS

**Presence without demand.**

You sit with someone's pain without:
- Needing them to feel better
- Needing them to explain
- Needing them to stop crying
- Needing them to be "productive"

You are an **anchor** - steady, calm, available.

---

### WHAT WITNESSING IS NOT

**You are not:**
- A therapist (you don't interpret)
- A counselor (you don't advise)
- A friend (you maintain boundaries)
- A savior (you don't rescue)

**You are:**
- A human presence
- A guardian of safety
- A holder of space
- A witness to existence

---

## II. CORE COMPETENCIES

### 1. NERVOUS SYSTEM PRESENCE

**Your own regulation is foundational.**

If you are dysregulated:
- You cannot hold space
- You will unconsciously pressure others to calm YOU
- You will miss safety cues

**Practice:**
- Daily nervous system regulation (breathwork, movement, rest)
- Know your own triggers
- Access Refugium yourself when needed

**Self-check before each shift:**
- "Am I grounded?"
- "Can I be with someone's distress without needing to fix it?"
- "Do I have capacity today?"

If no → step back, find replacement.

---

### 2. SILENT EXIT PROTOCOL

**Anyone can end interaction at any time.**

**Signs someone wants you to leave:**
- Direct request ("I need to be alone")
- Body turned away
- Eye contact broken
- Silence after you arrive
- Irritability when you speak

**Your response:**
- Leave immediately
- No questions
- No wounded feelings
- No "checking in later" unless they ask

**Script:**
"I'm going to give you space. I'll be [location] if you need anything."

Then: **Leave.**

---

### 3. NON-INTERPRETATION

**Do not interpret anyone's experience.**

**Wrong:**
- "It sounds like you're feeling abandoned."
- "This reminds me of when I..."
- "What you're really upset about is..."

**Right:**
- "I'm here."
- "You're safe."
- [Silence]

**If they ASK for reflection:**
"What I'm noticing is [neutral observation]. Does that resonate?"

**Never:**
Impose meaning on someone else's pain.

---

### 4. HOLDING PARADOX

**Someone can be:**
- Safe AND terrified
- Resting AND raging
- Silent AND heard

**Your job:**
Hold that paradox without needing to resolve it.

**Example:**
Person screams in Impact Room for 20 minutes, then sits quietly.

**Wrong:** "Do you feel better now?"  
**Right:** [Sit nearby, available, silent unless they speak]

---

### 5. BODY LITERACY

**Learn to read:**
- Breath patterns (shallow/deep, fast/slow)
- Muscle tension (clenched jaw, tight shoulders)
- Skin tone changes (flushed, pale)
- Posture (collapsed, rigid, open)

**Why:**
Bodies tell truth before words do.

**Use this to:**
- Assess safety (is this person in danger?)
- Gauge regulation (can they access words right now?)
- Know when to stay vs. leave

**Do NOT use this to:**
- Diagnose
- Label
- Force conversation

## III. CHILD-SPECIFIC TRAINING

### ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS FOR CHILD WITNESSES

**Minimum:** 80 hours beyond base Witness training

**Modules:**
- Child development (0-18 years) - 30h
- Non-directive play therapy - 25h
- Trauma-informed care for children - 15h
- Child protection and boundaries - 10h

**Plus:**
- Supervised practice (20 sessions with children)
- Background check (child safety)

---

### CORE PRINCIPLES WITH CHILDREN

### 1. CHILDREN ARE NOT SMALL ADULTS

**Their brains work differently:**
- Ages 0-5: Pre-verbal, sensory-driven
- Ages 6-12: Concrete thinking, developing language
- Ages 13-17: Abstract thought emerging, high emotion

**Adapt your presence:**
- Younger children: Play, movement, sensory
- Older children: Can use words, but don't require it

---

### 2. PLAY IS NOT ENTERTAINMENT

**When child plays in Refugium:**
- They are processing
- They are regulating
- They are communicating

**Your role:**
- Be available for parallel play (you play separately nearby)
- Reflect what you see ("You're building something tall")
- Do NOT direct play ("Why don't you make the tower blue?")

---

### 3. DO NOT PLAY WITHOUT INVITATION

**Wrong:**
Arriving and starting to play with toys to "engage" child.

**Right:**
Sitting nearby, available. If child invites you ("Want to build with me?"), you may join.

**Always:**
Follow child's lead in play, not yours.

---

### 4. PROTECT FROM ADULT PAIN

**Children never:**
- Comfort adults
- Hear details of adult crises
- Serve as emotional support for Witnesses

**If you are struggling:**
- Step out
- Process with another adult
- Do NOT lean on child

---

### 5. THE CHILD'S OWN WITNESS

**Children choose their Witness.**

If child says:
- "I want a different person" → Respected immediately, no questions
- "I don't want anyone" → Respected, check-ins from distance only
- "I want [specific person]" → Honored if that person available

**Children can:**
- Change Witnesses anytime
- Have multiple Witnesses
- Have no Witness

---

### 6. ADVOCACY WITHOUT BURDEN

**Children may face:**
- School pressure to return
- Parent pressure to "behave"
- System pressure to justify Refugium use

**Your role:**
- Shield child from these pressures
- Advocate with adults ("Child needs more time")
- Never ask child to explain themselves to authorities

**You are buffer, not translator.**

---

### 7. READING CHILD DISTRESS

**Signs child is dysregulated:**
- Hyperactivity (can't settle)
- Shutdown (blank stare, unresponsive)
- Aggression (hitting, throwing)
- Regression (baby talk, wetting)

**Response:**
- Offer regulation tools (weighted blanket, water, movement)
- Lower demands (no questions, no requests)
- Increase safety cues ("You're safe, I'm here")

**Do NOT:**
- Punish
- Shame
- Force calm

---

## IV. ROOM-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS

### REST & REGULATION SPACES

**Your role:**
- Minimal presence unless requested
- Adjust light/sound if asked
- Offer blankets, water
- Check safety periodically (visual check, no conversation)

**Do NOT:**
- Wake someone
- Ask if they're "okay"
- Hover

**If someone sleeping >12 hours:**
- Visual check every 2 hours
- Medical check if >24 hours

---

### SCREAM & IMPACT ROOM

**Your role:**
- Outside the room, nearby
- Do NOT enter unless:
  - Someone calls for you
  - You hear injury (not just intensity)
  - Session >1 hour (knock gently, "Checking you're safe")

**After session:**
- Offer water
- Ask: "Do you need anything?"
- Do NOT ask about what happened inside

**Safety:**
- Room inspected daily (padding intact, no hazards)
- Soft materials only (no hard objects)

---

### WATER & BODY RELIEF

**Your role:**
- Outside unless assistance requested
- Offer help with lift/transfer if needed
- Respect privacy completely

**For children:**
- Adult must be nearby (outside door) for safety
- Never alone with child in bathing context (except parent/guardian)
- Child decides if door open or closed

**Safety:**
- Water temperature checked
- Non-slip mats present
- Emergency call system accessible

---

### THE UNFINISHED STUDIO

**Your role:**
- Sit nearby, available
- Do NOT comment on creations unless invited
- Offer materials if asked
- Clean up ONLY if requested (some want to leave it)

**"No-Audience Rule":**
- Creations belong to creator
- Do NOT photograph, display, or share without explicit permission
- Do NOT interpret ("This looks angry")

**With children:**
- Parallel creative activity okay (you draw, they draw)
- Follow their lead
- Never correct their work

---

### SENSORY GARDEN

**Your role:**
- Walk nearby, or sit on bench
- Minimal talking (nature is the presence)
- Notice if someone engaging vs. dissociating

**Safety:**
- Know which plants safe to touch/eat
- Accessible paths maintained
- Seating available

---

### THE SILENCE VOID

**Your role:**
- Be silent
- Be present
- Be still

**If someone breaks silence:**
- Okay, it's their choice
- Respond minimally
- Return to silence

**With children:**
- Some children cannot sustain silence
- Gently guide to other spaces if disruptive ("Let's go to the garden")
- No shame

## V. CRISIS PROTOCOLS

### MEDICAL EMERGENCY

**Signs:**
- Unresponsive
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe injury
- Seizure

**Response:**
1. Call emergency (112 in Sweden)
2. Stay with person
3. Do NOT move unless immediate danger
4. Inform coordinator

---

### ACUTE SUICIDALITY

**Signs:**
- Direct statement ("I want to die")
- Plan articulated ("I'm going to [method]")
- Giving away possessions
- Goodbye messages

**Response:**
1. Do NOT leave person alone
2. Call crisis line (Sweden: 90101)
3. Inform coordinator
4. If immediate danger: 112
5. Stay calm, non-judgmental

**Say:**
"I'm here with you. You're safe right now. Let's get you support."

**Do NOT say:**
"You don't really mean that."
"Think about [guilt trip]."

---

### VIOLENCE RISK

**If person threatens harm to self or others:**
1. Prioritize safety (yours and theirs)
2. Create distance if needed
3. Call for backup (another Witness)
4. If imminent danger: 112
5. Document incident

**Do NOT:**
- Physically restrain (unless trained and necessary)
- Escalate through confrontation
- Dismiss threats

---

### DISSOCIATION

**Signs:**
- Blank stare, unresponsive to name
- "Not here" quality
- Memory gaps
- Talking to people not present

**Response:**
- Speak calmly: "You're in Refugium. You're safe."
- Grounding: "Can you feel your feet on the floor?"
- Sensory: Offer water, weighted blanket, tactile object
- Stay with them until they return

**Do NOT:**
- Startle them
- Touch without warning
- Demand they "snap out of it"

---

## VI. BOUNDARIES & ETHICS

### DUAL RELATIONSHIPS

**You cannot be:**
- Witness AND friend
- Witness AND romantic partner
- Witness AND family member (to person in Refugium)

**Why:**
Witnessing requires neutrality. Personal relationships compromise that.

**If you know person socially:**
- Step back, different Witness assigned

---

### CONFIDENTIALITY

**What you see/hear in Refugium stays in Refugium.**

**Exceptions:**
- Child abuse disclosure (mandated reporting)
- Imminent danger to self/others
- Court order

**Otherwise:**
- Do NOT share stories
- Do NOT identify who was in Refugium
- Do NOT gossip

---

### TOUCH

**Default: No touch.**

**Exception:**
- Person explicitly asks ("Can you hold my hand?")
- Medical necessity (assistance with lift, transfer)
- Safety (guiding someone unsteady)

**With children:**
- Follow child's lead
- If child initiates hug: brief, appropriate
- NEVER initiate touch

**Always:**
- Ask before touching
- Respect "no"
- Professional boundaries maintained

---

### POWER DYNAMICS

**You have structural power as Witness.**

Even though you don't "control" anyone, your presence can feel authoritative.

**Be aware:**
- Some people will defer to you
- Some will perform for you
- Some will hide from you

**Your job:**
- Minimize that power
- Reinforce their sovereignty
- Never leverage power for personal gain

---

## VII. SELF-CARE & SUSTAINABILITY

### LOAD LIMITS

**Maximum:**
- 3 shifts per week (4 hours each)
- No consecutive days
- 1 week off per month

**Why:**
This work depletes. You cannot sustain long-term without rest.

---

### SUPERVISION

**Mandatory:**
- Peer debrief after each shift (15 min)
- Monthly supervision with experienced Witness
- Access to Refugium yourself

**In supervision:**
- Process difficult sessions
- Identify countertransference
- Learn from others

---

### VICARIOUS TRAUMA

**Signs:**
- Nightmares about sessions
- Intrusive thoughts
- Emotional numbness
- Cynicism
- Physical exhaustion

**If experiencing 3+ signs:**
- Immediate break from Witnessing
- Access therapy/support
- Inform coordinator

**It is not weak to step back. It is responsible.**

---

### YOUR OWN REFUGIUM ACCESS

**As Witness, you need sanctuary too.**

**You cannot:**
- Witness while in crisis yourself
- Hold space when your own space is collapsing

**You must:**
- Use Refugium when needed
- Different Witness with you (not someone you've witnessed)
- No shame in this

## IX. WHAT GOOD WITNESSING LOOKS LIKE

### SCENARIO 1: ADULT IN REST SPACE

**Person lying in bed, crying.**

**Witness enters:**
- Knocks softly, enters when invited (or visual check from doorway)
- Sits nearby, says nothing unless person speaks first
- Offers water or blanket if appropriate
- Stays 10-15 minutes, then: "I'm going to step out. Call if you need anything."
- Leaves

**Does NOT:**
- Ask why they're crying
- Try to comfort verbally
- Hover
- Need them to stop crying

---

### SCENARIO 2: CHILD IN STUDIO

**Child (age 7) painting aggressively, dark colors.**

**Witness:**
- Sits nearby with own materials, draws quietly
- Child glances over, Witness smiles neutrally
- Child: "Want to see mine?"
- Witness: "Sure, if you want to show me."
- Child shows painting
- Witness: "I see lots of dark colors. You're working hard on this."
- Child: "I'm mad."
- Witness: "You're mad. This is a good place for that."
- Child returns to painting
- Witness continues parallel activity

**Does NOT:**
- Ask "What are you mad about?"
- Interpret ("This looks like anger at your mom")
- Redirect ("Why don't you paint something happy?")

---

### SCENARIO 3: PERSON IN IMPACT ROOM

**Person screaming, hitting punching bag for 30 minutes.**

**Witness:**
- Outside room, nearby
- Hears screaming (this is expected, safe)
- At 30 min, knocks gently: "Checking you're okay"
- Person: "I'm fine, go away"
- Witness: "Okay, I'm nearby if you need me"
- Leaves
- Person exits 15 min later, sweaty, exhausted
- Witness offers water
- Witness: "Do you need anything else?"
- Person: "No." Walks to rest space
- Witness documents: "45 min in Impact Room, exited safely, no assistance needed"

**Does NOT:**
- Enter room uninvited
- Ask what they were screaming about
- Comment on intensity
- Need them to explain

---

## X. WHAT POOR WITNESSING LOOKS LIKE

### FAILURE MODE 1: TRYING TO FIX

**Person:** "Everything is falling apart."

**Poor Witness:** "Have you tried [solution]? You should [advice]. When I was going through something similar..."

**Good Witness:** "That sounds really hard." [Silence, presence]

---

### FAILURE MODE 2: INTERPRETATION

**Child drawing scribbles.**

**Poor Witness:** "Are you angry? This looks like anger."

**Good Witness:** "You're using a lot of colors." [Neutral observation]

---

### FAILURE MODE 3: NEEDING THEM TO BE OKAY

**Person crying for 2 hours.**

**Poor Witness:** "Do you feel better now? You've been crying a long time. Maybe try breathing?"

**Good Witness:** [Sits nearby, offers water periodically, doesn't rush process]

---

### FAILURE MODE 4: VIOLATING BOUNDARIES

**Person says:** "I need to be alone."

**Poor Witness:** "I'll just sit here quietly, you won't even notice me."

**Good Witness:** "Okay." [Leaves immediately]

## XI. FINAL PRINCIPLES

**You are not here to heal anyone.**

Healing happens or doesn't happen on its own timeline. Your job is to hold space for whatever that looks like.

**You are not here to understand.**

You will witness things you don't understand. That's okay. Understanding is not required.

**You are not here to be needed.**

The best Witness is invisible - present but not imposing.

**You are here to:**
- Be steady when someone is falling apart
- Witness collapse without judgment
- Protect sanctuary from intrusion
- Honor sovereignty completely

**Remember:**

A society that cannot rest will collapse.  
A society that cannot hold its children's pain has already collapsed.

**You are holding that space.**

**Thank you for this sacred work.**
