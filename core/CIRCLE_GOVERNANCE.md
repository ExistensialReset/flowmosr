## PURPOSE

> **"Most decisions in Flow happen in Circles. Not in LOTUS. Not automatically. In rooms with people talking."**

This is how the **8%** actually works.

- **90%** of coordination: Automatic (RIS signals, production feedback, no meeting needed)
- **8%** of coordination: **Circle decisions** (this document)
- **2%** of coordination: LOTUS (constitutional, conflicts, emergency)

**Circle governance is everyday democracy.**

---

## WHAT IS A CIRCLE?

A Circle is 5-30 people who:
- Share resources or space
- Work together regularly
- Need to make decisions together
- See each other face-to-face (or video)

**Types:**
- **Micro-Circle:** 2-5 friends sharing informally
- **Baseline Circle:** 10-30 people securing Baseline together
- **Work Circle:** People doing same type of work (garden Circle, tech Circle, care Circle)
- **Geographic Circle:** People living in same building/area
- **Project Circle:** Temporary group for specific project

**All use similar decision methods.**

---

## WHAT CIRCLES DECIDE

### Circles Handle:

**Daily Coordination:**
- Work schedules (who does what when)
- Resource allocation within Circle quota (food, tools, space)
- Task division (fairly distributed)
- Quality standards (how we do this work)

**Space & Resources:**
- How we use our shared space
- What equipment to acquire
- Maintenance priorities
- Storage organization

**Interpersonal:**
- Schedule conflicts (two people want same resource)
- Work style differences (person A likes planning, person B likes spontaneity)
- Communication norms (meeting frequency, how we give feedback)
- Minor friction (see FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md)

**Circle Culture:**
- Meeting times/frequency
- Who facilitates
- How we celebrate
- Rituals and traditions

### Circles DON'T Handle:

**Escalate to Inter-Circle Coordination:**
- Decisions affecting multiple Circles
- Resource allocation between Circles
- Conflicts between Circles

**Escalate to LOTUS:**
- Constitutional issues
- Major infrastructure (affects whole Node)
- Serious harm/accountability (see FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md)
- Baseline violations

**Escalate to Experts:**
- Specialized technical decisions
- Healthcare decisions
- Legal questions
- (But Circle still decides whether to follow expert advice)

## HOW CIRCLES DECIDE

### Meeting Structure

**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly (Circle decides)

**Duration:** 1-2 hours typical

**Who Attends:**
- All Circle members invited
- Participation voluntary (but encouraged)
- Quorum: 50%+ for major decisions

**Facilitator:**
- **Rotates** (everyone takes turn)
- **Role:** Guide discussion, not decide outcome
- **Training:** 30-60 min facilitation basics
- **Rotation:** Weekly, monthly, or per-meeting

---

## DECISION METHODS

### Method 1: CONSENSUS (Preferred)

**Definition:** Everyone agrees or stands aside

**Process:**

**1. Proposal presented** (anyone can propose)
- What are we deciding?
- Why?
- What's the proposal?

**2. Clarifying questions**
- Not debate, just understanding
- "What do you mean by X?"
- "How would this work?"

**3. Concerns raised**
- "I'm worried about Y"
- "This might cause Z"
- "What about people who..."

**4. Proposal adjusted**
- Based on concerns
- Improved together
- May look different than original

**5. Consent check**
- Facilitator: "Any objections?"
- **Consent ≠ Everyone loves it**
- **Consent = No one has a blocking concern**

**6. Decision made**
- If no objections: Approved by consensus
- If objection: More discussion or try Method 2

**Positions in consensus:**

- **Agree:** "I support this fully"
- **Stand aside:** "Not my preference, but won't block" (decision still passes)
- **Object:** "I have a serious concern that must be addressed" (blocks decision)

**When to use:** Most Circle decisions

---

### Method 2: MAJORITY VOTE (When Consensus Fails)

**When to use:**
- Consensus tried but can't reach it
- Time-sensitive decision
- Multiple good options (just pick one)

**Thresholds:**

**Simple majority (>50%):**
- Routine decisions
- Reversible choices
- Low stakes

**Supermajority (66%+):**
- Major changes to Circle structure
- Budget decisions
- Changes to decision-making process itself

**Process:**

**1. Discussion** (same as consensus steps 1-4)

**2. Vote**
- Show of hands (or anonymous paper if sensitive)
- Count clearly
- Record result

**3. Decision made**
- Majority wins
- Minority opinion noted in record
- Everyone commits to try it (even if voted against)

**4. Review later**
- Bad decision? Circle can reverse it
- Good decision? Keep it

**When to use:** When consensus can't be reached after genuine attempt

---

### Method 3: DELEGATE (For Specialized/Routine Decisions)

**When to use:**
- Technical decision requiring expertise
- Routine decision (same every week)
- Time-sensitive (can't wait for meeting)

**Process:**

**1. Circle authorizes person/small group**
- "Garden Circle delegates to Maya: decide which seeds to order"
- Clear mandate
- Clear boundaries (budget, principles)
- Time limit

**2. Person decides**
- Within mandate
- Reports back to Circle

**3. Circle can revoke**
- If unhappy with delegation
- Return to Circle decision-making

**Examples:**
- "Tech person decides which software for task X"
- "Care Circle member decides menu for week"
- "Facilitator decides meeting time this week"

**When to use:** Efficiency without losing Circle oversight

## CONFLICT IN CIRCLES

### Types of Conflict

**Level 1: Disagreement**
- People have different preferences
- Normal, healthy, expected
- Handle through discussion + decision methods above

**Level 2: Friction**
- Repeated small conflicts
- Pattern emerging
- Handle through Circle discussion + mediation

**Level 3: Disruption**
- One person repeatedly blocking consensus
- Or dominating discussion
- Or ignoring decisions
- Handle through accountability process (see FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md)

### Conflict Resolution Tools

**1. Talking Piece**
- Object passed around
- Only person holding it speaks
- Everyone gets turn
- Slows down heated discussion

**2. Cooling-Off Period**
- "Let's discuss next week, not today"
- Emotions settle
- People reflect

**3. Mediation**
- Third person (not in conflict) facilitates
- Can be from same Circle or different Circle
- Neutral, doesn't take sides

**4. Restorative Circle**
- If harm occurred
- Full Circle process
- See FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md

**5. Exit**
- If conflict can't resolve
- Person can leave Circle
- See EXIT_AND_COMPOST.md

## FACILITATION BASICS

### Facilitator's Job:

**Before Meeting:**
- Create agenda (or ask Circle for items)
- Ensure space is ready
- Remind people of meeting time

**During Meeting:**
- Welcome everyone
- Go through agenda
- **Manage speaking time** (everyone gets to speak, no one dominates)
- **Track time** (keep meeting on schedule)
- **Summarize decisions** ("So we decided X, right?")
- **Note action items** (who does what by when)

**After Meeting:**
- Write brief summary
- Post to Circle communication channel
- Follow up on action items

### What Facilitator Does NOT Do:

- Decide outcome
- Argue for their position during facilitation (step down from role to speak personally)
- Control the group
- Take sides in conflict

**Facilitation is service, not power.**

## DOCUMENTATION

### What Gets Written Down:

**Decision Record (Brief):**
```
DATE: March 19, 2026
DECISION: Garden Circle will plant tomatoes this year (not peppers)
METHOD: Majority vote (8 yes, 3 no)
REASONING: Better suited to our climate, easier to preserve
ACTION: Maya orders seeds by March 25
REVIEW: August (see if it worked)
```

**Keep it short.** 3-5 sentences max.

**Where:** Shared document, Circle ledger, or Node system

### What Doesn't Need Writing:

- Every discussion detail
- Who said what
- Routine task assignments everyone already knows

**Write enough to remember. No more.**

## PARTICIPATION

### Expected:

- Attend most meetings (60%+ attendance)
- Speak when you have input
- Listen when others speak
- Follow through on commitments
- Respect decisions (even if you voted against)

### NOT Expected:

- 100% attendance (life happens)
- Speaking at every meeting (quiet people exist)
- Loving every decision
- Never disagreeing

### If Participation Drops:

**Circle checks in:**
- "Hey, you've missed 5 meetings. Everything OK?"
- Maybe: Person overwhelmed, needs support
- Maybe: Person disengaged, should exit
- Maybe: Meetings at bad time, need to adjust

**Response depends on cause.**

## WHEN TO ESCALATE

### To Inter-Circle Coordination:

**When:**
- Decision affects multiple Circles
- Circles disagree on shared resource
- Need Node-wide input

**How:**
- Send representative to coordination meeting
- Or request LOTUS mediation (rare)

**Example:** Garden Circle and Kitchen Circle both want the greenhouse space

### To LOTUS:

**When:**
- Constitutional issue
- Circle decision violates Axiom
- Serious harm/accountability
- Can't resolve through coordination

**How:**
- Request LOTUS panel via Node process
- See LOTUS_GOVERNANCE_PROTOCOL_v2.md

**Example:** Circle decision would reduce someone's Baseline access

### To Experts:

**When:**
- Specialized knowledge needed
- Technical decision beyond Circle expertise

**How:**
- Request expert analysis (see EXPERT_CERTIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v2.md)
- Circle decides whether to follow advice

**Example:** Healthcare decision, legal question, structural engineering

**Circle always retains decision authority** (even after expert input)

## PRACTICAL TEMPLATES

### Simple Meeting Agenda

```
CIRCLE: Garden Circle
DATE: March 19, 2026
FACILITATOR: Maya
TIME: 60 minutes

1. Check-in (5 min) - How is everyone?
2. Review last meeting action items (5 min)
3. Decision: What to plant this year? (30 min)
4. Discussion: Compost system improvement ideas (15 min)
5. Action items + next meeting (5 min)
```

### Decision Record Template

```
DECISION: [What we decided]
DATE: [When]
METHOD: [Consensus / Majority vote / Delegated]
REASONING: [Why - 1-2 sentences]
ACTION: [Who does what by when]
REVIEW: [When we check if this worked]
```

## CIRCLE SIZE & COMPLEXITY

### Small Circle (5-10 people):

- **Informal** (can decide things in hallway conversation)
- **Consensus easy** (fewer people = easier agreement)
- **Meetings brief** (30-60 min)
- **Low documentation** (everyone remembers)

### Medium Circle (10-20 people):

- **More structure needed** (agenda, facilitator, notes)
- **Consensus harder** (more diverse opinions)
- **Meetings longer** (60-90 min)
- **Documentation important** (people forget)

### Large Circle (20-30 people):

- **Formal structure required**
- **Consider breaking into smaller Circles** (work groups)
- **Coordination layer** (representatives meet)
- **May need occasional LOTUS** (if can't reach decisions)

**If Circle >30 people regularly:**
- Split into smaller Circles OR
- Transition to LOTUS for major decisions
- See SMALL_POOL_LOTUS_PROTOCOLS.md

## FAILURE MODES & RESPONSES

### 1. One Person Dominates

**Signs:**
- Same person speaks 50%+ of meeting
- Others quiet
- Decisions reflect one person's preferences

**Response:**
- Facilitator enforces speaking time limits
- "You've spoken 3 times, let others go first"
- Talking piece
- Anonymous voting for sensitive decisions

### 2. Chronic Low Participation

**Signs:**
- Only 3-5 people attend (out of 15)
- Same people every time
- Decisions made by minority

**Response:**
- Survey non-attendees (why not coming?)
- Change meeting time/format
- Reduce frequency (maybe weekly too much)
- Accept some non-participation (not everyone wants to govern)

### 3. Endless Discussion, No Decision

**Signs:**
- Same topic for 3+ meetings
- No progress
- Frustration building

**Response:**
- Set time limit: "We decide TODAY"
- If consensus fails, use majority vote
- Or delegate to expert/small group
- Or escalate to LOTUS if constitutional issue

### 4. Decisions Ignored

**Signs:**
- Circle decides X, people do Y
- No follow-through on action items
- Decisions feel meaningless

**Response:**
- Accountability conversation (gentle first)
- If pattern: See FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md
- If systemic: Check if decisions realistic (maybe unrealistic expectations)

### 5. Consensus Abused

**Signs:**
- One person blocks everything
- Or group pressures person to drop objection
- Consensus becomes tyranny of minority OR majority

**Response:**
- Discuss consent vs preference (objection must be serious concern, not "I don't prefer this")
- If repeated blocking: Accountability conversation
- If needed: Switch to majority vote for that decision
- Re-train on consensus method

## INTEGRATION WITH OTHER SYSTEMS

### With Automatic Coordination (90%)

**Circle doesn't decide:**
- What to produce (production feedback shows this)
- Who needs resources (RIS signals this)
- Prices (no prices in Flow)

**Circle DOES decide:**
- HOW to respond to signals (which method, which people)
- Quality standards
- Work distribution

**Example:** RIS shows tomato scarcity → Circle decides to plant tomatoes (not peppers)

### With LOTUS (2%)

**LOTUS doesn't override Circle decisions** unless:
- Constitutional violation
- Baseline violation
- Serious harm

**Circle can request LOTUS mediation** for conflicts

### With Expert Certification

**Experts provide analysis**
**Circle decides whether to follow**

**Exception:** Safety-critical decisions (structural engineering, healthcare emergency) where expert veto may apply

### With Justice System

**Minor conflicts:** Circle handles via restorative conversation

**Serious harm:** Escalates to accountability Circle or LOTUS

**See:** FLOW_JUSTICE_PRINCIPLES.md

## ASSESSMENT QUESTIONS

### Can Your Circle Answer These?

**Decision-Making:**
1. Do you try consensus first?
2. Do you have clear fallback to majority vote?
3. Can you delegate routine decisions?
4. Do you document major decisions?
5. Do you review decisions later (did it work)?

**Participation:**
6. Does everyone get chance to speak?
7. Is attendance reasonable (>50% for major decisions)?
8. Can people disagree without drama?
9. Do quiet people feel heard?
10. Is facilitation rotated fairly?

**Conflict:**
11. Can you handle disagreements constructively?
12. Do you have mediation process?
13. Are repeated conflicts addressed?
14. Can people exit gracefully if needed?
15. Is there accountability without punishment?

**Escalation:**
16. Do you know when to escalate to inter-Circle coordination?
17. Do you know when to escalate to LOTUS?
18. Do you know when to consult experts?
19. Do you retain decision authority after expert input?
20. Can you request help without shame?

**If you can answer "yes" to 17+, your Circle governance is healthy.**

## FINAL PRINCIPLE

> **"Circles are where democracy lives. Not in constitutions. Not in systems. In rooms with people talking."**

**The best governance is:**
- Simple enough anyone can participate
- Structured enough to work when conflict arises
- Flexible enough to adapt to context
- Human enough to forgive mistakes

**Circle governance is not perfect.**  
**It's just better than the alternatives.**
