# RESOURCE_METRIC_STANDARDS VERSION HISTORY

## Version 1.7 — The Unified Baseline
**Status:** Operational Specification  
**Authors:** Elinor Frejd & Gemini & Claude  

- Focus on water, physical, and social Baseline  
- Minimum 50L/person/day water for drinking, hygiene, and food preparation  
- Climate-adapted thermal Baseline  
- Access to medical technology and mobility aids  
- Baseline for hygiene, local chemistry, and biochemical resources  
- Category of Joy: spices, sweets, fermented foods  
- Social Baseline: minimum 3 meaningful social interactions per week  
- Internet and high-frequency public transit guaranteed  

---

## Version 2.0
**Key Changes:**  
- Introduced food and energy metrics: initial 2,200 kcal/day for adults  
- Age-adjusted energy for children  
- Clearer definitions for electricity and energy access  
- Baseline resources tied to rights, not coercion  

---

## Version 3.0 — Global Baseline Standard
**Status:** Planetary, LOTUS majority required for modification  
- Full SI-unit-based Baseline  
- Metrics for food, water, air, temperature, electricity, light, habitable space, time, and information  
- Baseline per individual, climate-independent  
- Verification via self-declaration, peer review, sensors, LOTUS vote in case of dispute  
- Non-revocability clause  
- Revision only through global LOTUS majority  

---

## Version 4.0
**Key Changes:**  
- Weight-based caloric requirements: 20–90 kg per individual, adjusted for physical and mental activity  
- Children and youth travel: minimum two extended trips (train or boat) between ages 7–25  
- Mass transit included as free access for all  
- Electricity: additional allocation if medical needs require it  
- Separation between food Baseline and travel as its own chapter  

---

## Version 5.0
**Key Changes:**  
- Consolidated version of v4.0 with clarifications:  
  - Weight-based energy requirements for children and adults  
  - Activity- and work-adjusted energy  
  - Children's and youth travel (7–25 years) as a dedicated Baseline chapter  
  - Mass transit and public transportation guaranteed free  
  - Electricity for medical or life-sustaining equipment  
  - Psychological and social Baseline maintained as separate chapter  

**Principle:** Baseline = floor, never ceiling. No human may fall below structural minimums for survival, dignity, and cognitive liberty.
