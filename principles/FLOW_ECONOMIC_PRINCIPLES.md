## I. CORE PRINCIPLES (TIER 1 - INVIOLABLE)

### 1. NO CURRENCY

- Money, debt, and prices eliminated  
- Direct resource allocation based on need and availability  
- Resource tracking, contribution hours, social recognition, voluntary participation replace currency  

---

### 2. BASELINE FIRST

- Priority: Baseline → Infrastructure → Essential Services → Community Projects → Surplus  
- Baseline can never be suspended, withheld, or traded  

**Inter-Node Balance Signaling:**  
- Persistent imbalance visible → dialogue → support/adjustment strategies co-developed  
- Escalation if dialogue fails:  
  1. Temporary reduction in access to **non-essential resources** (resources above Baseline, excluding infrastructure maintenance & essential services)  
  2. Limited restriction on non-critical inter-Node exchanges  
  3. Baseline never affected  

---

### 3. RESOURCE TRACKING (AGGREGATED, ANONYMOUS)

- Totals tracked per Node (food, energy, water, residents, service availability)  
- No individual tracking  
- Local, regional, and global aggregation only  
- Individual entries deleted after 30 days  

---

### 4. CONTRIBUTION, NOT COMPENSATION

#### 4.1 PARTICIPATION REALITY

- Participation voluntary, system designed to function under partial/uneven engagement  
- Structural redundancy and rotation maintain stability  

#### 4.2 SYSTEM CAPACITY THRESHOLD

- Minimum contribution required for Baseline  
- If below threshold:  
  1. Capacity strain signal  
  2. Non-essential services scale down  
  3. Rotation frequency increases  
  4. **Emergency contribution phase (voluntary)**  
     - Signaling only, no coercion  
     - Volunteers encouraged  
     - Insufficient volunteers → further non-essential services scaled down  
     - Baseline never affected  

**Burden Balancing Principle:** Unpleasant tasks evenly distributed, time-bounded, reduced via design  
**Engagement Decay Principle:** Motivation fluctuates, system resilient via redundancy & structural adaptation  

---

### 5. SURPLUS REDISTRIBUTION (NO HOARDING)

- Surplus to reserves, inter-Node sharing, community abundance  
- Persistent hoarding triggers: >3 documented incidents within 6 months after 2 verbal reminders  
- Governance adjustment: reduced influence (e.g., voting weight -50% or temporary ineligibility for LOTUS panels)  
- Appeal: affected person may appeal to LOTUS or neutral external panel within 30 days  
- During appeal, reduced influence temporarily suspended unless safety concerns exist  

---

### 6. INTER-NODE EXCHANGE (NEED-BASED, NOT PROFIT)

- Exchange based on need, not profit  
- Aggregate flows tracked, persistent imbalance flagged  
- Escalation: temporary limitation of non-critical exchanges, Baseline never affected  

---

### 7. LABOR STRUCTURE (ESSENTIAL WORK GUARANTEED)

- Fully rotating tasks → everyone participates  
- Semi-legit professions → partial rotation  
- Legit professions → protected from overload  

**Specialist Redundancy Rule:**  
- 2–3 trained per critical role OR access to regional specialist pool (e.g., for all except most critical like doctors, psychologists, engineers)  
- Cost: ~1.5h/person/year for training & ongoing practice  
- Regional support triggered if local redundancy falls below threshold  

---

### 8. REPAIR CULTURE

- Durable, repairable, long-lasting design  
- Modular schematics, tool libraries  

---

## II. JUSTICE INFRASTRUCTURE FINANCING

- Justice essential (Priority 3)  
- Cost: 680h/year human time + ~$90/year technology (~5.3% of Node operating cost)  
- Compensation: contribution + Baseline + social status  

---

## III. OPERATIONAL MECHANISMS

- Resource flow: Baseline direct access; regional balancing via reciprocity  
- Hour tracking: for capacity/fairness, never for payment  
- Free rider management: social interventions only

### APPENDIX B: SOCIAL STRUCTURE REALITY PATCH

- Nodes ~500 people → clusters 10–50  
- Accountability: Local cluster → Cross-cluster → System-level LOTUS  
- Anti-capture: rotation + random selection; pattern detection flags repeated concentration/dominance  

---

### APPENDIX C: TRUST DECAY OVER TIME PATCH

- Trust naturally decays → system maintains **trust floor**  
- Regeneration: structural fairness, rotation, LOTUS oversight, Refugium, Lyceum  
- Early warning signals: participation <50% 3 months, ≥3 reports of exhaustion/conflict  
- Response: Node reflection, temporary reduction non-essential load, increased transparency  
- Escalation: procedural review and LOTUS involvement
