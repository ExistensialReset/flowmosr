## PURPOSE

> "Economy means 'household management.' We manage the household of Earth."

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. No Currency (No Money, No Debt, No Prices)

- No buying/selling, no wages, no wealth accumulation.
- Replaced by direct resource allocation and contribution tracking.

---

### 2. Baseline First (Survival Before Surplus)

1. **Baseline Allocation** (ALWAYS FIRST: Food, Water, Energy, Shelter, Health).
2. **Infrastructure Maintenance**.
3. **Essential Services**.
4. **Community Projects**.
5. **Surplus Distribution**.

---

### 3. Resource Tracking (Aggregated, Anonymous)

- We track total production and consumption of the system.
- We do NOT track individual consumption or performance.
- System is transparent; individuals are private.

---

### 4. Contribution, Not Compensation

- Everyone gets Baseline regardless of work.
- Contribution is voluntary and varied (care work = direct production).
- Recognition through social status and expertise, not money.

---

### 5. Surplus Redistribution (No Hoarding)

- Surplus goes to regional reserves, inter-Node sharing, or community abundance.
- Individual hoarding is not allowed.

---

### 6. Inter-Node Exchange (Need-Based)

- "You need this, I have extra. I need that, you have extra."
- No calculation of "equivalence" or profit extraction.

---

### 7. Labor Structure (Essential Work Guaranteed)

- **Rotation:** All adults rotate through essential work (civic duty).
- **Specialization:** Specialists (doctors, engineers) are exempt but protected from burnout.

---

### 8. No Planned Obsolescence (Repair Culture)

- Modular design, repairability, and durability.
- Tool libraries and repair workshops at every Node.
