# I. CORE PRINCIPLES (TIER 1 - INVIOLABLE)

---

## 1. RESTORATION OVER PUNISHMENT

**Principle:**  
Justice exists to restore relationships, safety, and system integrity — not to punish individuals.

**Rationale:**  
Punitive systems escalate harm and fail to address root causes.

**Implementation:**
- Focus on repair, not retribution  
- Harm addressed through dialogue, accountability, and restoration  
- Structural causes examined alongside individual actions  

---

## 2. SAFETY FIRST

**Principle:**  
Perceived and actual safety is a prerequisite for all justice processes.

**Critical rule:**
> No restorative process proceeds if participants feel unsafe.

---

## 3. BASELINE PROTECTION

**Principle:**  
Justice can never remove access to baseline resources.

**Non-negotiable:**
- No denial of food, shelter, healthcare, or safety  
- No coercion through survival dependency  

---

## 4. PROCESS OVER OUTCOME

**Principle:**  
A fair process is more important than a forced resolution.

**Implementation:**
- Participants must be heard  
- Processes must be transparent  
- Outcomes must emerge from integrity, not pressure  

---

## 5. DISTRIBUTED AUTHORITY

**Principle:**  
Justice authority must be distributed to prevent concentration of power.

**Mechanisms:**
- Rotating facilitators  
- Multi-person panels (e.g. LOTUS)  
- Separation between detection, review, and appeal  

---

## 6. HUMAN COMPLEXITY ACKNOWLEDGED

**Principle:**  
People are not fixed as “good” or “bad.”

**Implementation:**
- Context matters  
- Behavior patterns matter more than isolated incidents  
- Capacity for change is assumed but not forced

# II. JUSTICE STRUCTURE

---

## 2.1 THREE-LAYER JUSTICE MODEL

### Layer 1: SOCIAL RESOLUTION
- Direct conversation  
- Mediated dialogue  
- Informal repair  

---

### Layer 2: STRUCTURED RESTORATION
- Facilitated sessions  
- Circle processes  
- Defined agreements and follow-up  

---

### Layer 3: FORMAL REVIEW (LOTUS OR EQUIVALENT)
- Multi-person review panel  
- Cross-cluster or cross-Node participation  
- Used for complex, repeated, or escalated cases  

---

## 2.2 ROLE TYPES

- **Facilitators** — guide process  
- **Human Mirrors** — reflect emotional and relational dynamics  
- **Child Witness (if applicable)** — represent vulnerable perspectives  
- **Observers (optional)** — ensure transparency and fairness  

---

## 2.3 APPEAL STRUCTURE

- Independent from initial review  
- Time-bounded  
- Accessible to affected individuals  

---

# IV. STABILITY & ANTI-COLLAPSE PROTOCOLS

---

## J.1 SAFETY OVERRIDE PROTOCOL (SOP)

**Purpose:**  
Ensure immediate safety independent of process timelines.

### Trigger:
- Participant reports feeling unsafe  
- Ongoing harm or escalation risk  

### Immediate Actions:
- Temporary separation  
- Activity restriction if needed  
- Rapid support allocation  

### Constraints:
- Non-punitive  
- Baseline unaffected  
- Review within 72 hours  

**Principle:**
> Safety precedes process.

---

## J.2 PERCEIVED FAIRNESS PROTOCOL (PFP)

**Purpose:**  
Ensure justice is trusted, not just correct.

### After resolution:
Participants are asked:
> “Do you feel this process was fair?”

### If NO:
- Process review triggered  
- Optional re-facilitation  

**Principle:**
> Justice must be experienced as fair.

---

## J.3 MANIPULATION RESILIENCE PROTOCOL (MRP)

**Purpose:**  
Prevent dominance through charisma or narrative control.

### Mechanisms:
- Structured speaking order  
- Time limits  
- Optional anonymized input  

### Detection:
- Dominant voice patterns  
- Withdrawal of others  

### Response:
- Facilitation reset  
- Format adjustment  

---

## J.4 REPEATED HARM ESCALATION (RHE)

**Purpose:**  
Address patterns, not just incidents.

### Trigger:
- 3+ similar incidents within 6 months  

### Response:
- Pattern-based review  
- Expanded panel  
- Structural adjustments (role limits, boundaries)  

**Principle:**
> Patterns require structural response.

---

## J.5 SAFE REPORTING CHANNEL (SRC)

**Purpose:**  
Enable early, low-risk reporting.

### Features:
- Confidential input  
- No requirement for formal accusation  

### Function:
- Pattern detection  
- Early intervention  

**Principle:**
> Silence is a system failure.

---

## J.6 RESTORATIVE BOUNDARY PROTOCOL (RBP)

**Purpose:**  
Define limits of restorative processes.

### Applies when:
- Ongoing harm continues  
- Refusal to engage  
- Severe safety breach  

### Response:
- Protective measures  
- External review  
- Parallel restoration (not sole method)  

**Principle:**
> Restoration is primary, not exclusive.

## ⚖️ JUSTICE–GOVERNANCE INTERFACE LAYER

**Purpose:**  
Define how Justice processes and Governance structures interact, without creating power concentration or ambiguity.

---

### 1. SEPARATION OF FUNCTIONS

Justice and Governance serve different roles:

- **Justice:** Handles conflict, harm, and process fairness  
- **Governance:** Handles decisions, direction, and coordination  

**Rule:**
> Governance cannot override Justice processes.  
> Justice cannot make policy decisions.

---

### 2. DECISION IMPACT VS DECISION AUTHORITY

Justice may affect **participation conditions**, but not **system direction**.

Examples:

- Justice MAY:
  - Temporarily reduce governance participation weight  
  - Restrict access to specific roles  
  - Require structural boundaries  

- Justice may NOT:
  - Set policy  
  - Allocate resources  
  - Override collective decisions  

---

### 3. CONDITIONAL PARTICIPATION PRINCIPLE

Participation in governance is:

> **Universal by default, conditional by process**

Meaning:

- All individuals have equal voice initially  
- Justice processes may temporarily adjust participation conditions  
- Adjustments must be:
  - Time-bounded  
  - Reviewable  
  - Appealable  

---

### 4. APPEAL PATHWAY (STRUCTURAL)

All Justice decisions that affect governance participation must allow:

- Appeal to **independent LOTUS panel** OR  
- External neutral review body  

**Constraints:**

- Time-bounded (e.g. ≤30 days)  
- Separate from original decision-makers  

---

### 5. SYSTEM-LEVEL FEEDBACK LOOP

Justice outputs must inform Governance:

- Repeated conflicts → governance redesign  
- Power concentration signals → structural adjustment  
- Trust decay → governance simplification  

**Mechanism:**

- Periodic “Justice → Governance signal reports”  
- No individual exposure, only pattern-level data  

---

### 6. GOVERNANCE NON-INTERFERENCE RULE

Governance bodies (including LOTUS) must not:

- Interfere in active justice processes  
- Influence case outcomes  
- Protect individuals from process  

**Exception:**
- Only through formal appeal structures  

---

### 7. SYSTEM OVERSIGHT (META-LAYER)

To prevent system drift:

- Governance may initiate:
  - Process audits  
  - Structural reviews  

But:

- Must not interfere in active cases  
- Must operate on **system-level, not case-level**

---

### 8. FAILURE SAFEGUARD

If conflict emerges between Justice and Governance:

1. Pause non-essential governance decisions  
2. Activate independent review panel  
3. Prioritize:
   - Safety  
   - Process integrity  
   - Trust preservation  

---

### PRINCIPLE

> Justice protects fairness.  
> Governance enables coordination.  
> Neither must absorb the other.
