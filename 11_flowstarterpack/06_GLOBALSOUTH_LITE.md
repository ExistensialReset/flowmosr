# 🌍 Global South Pathway (Condensed)

This is not a blueprint.  
It must be adapted locally.

## LEVEL 0 — Survival Circle (Week 1)
5–15 people share:
- Meals  
- Water collection  
- Childcare  
- Tools  
- Night safety  

## LEVEL 1 — Water First - once a reliable water source and storage system is established
- Rainwater harvesting, biosand filters, protected wells.
Water initiatives often benefit from women’s leadership and local knowledge. 

## LEVEL 2 — Food Security - once core food systems are producing consistently
Community garden, seed saving, chickens, drought-resistant crops.

## LEVEL 3 — Economic Resilience 
ROSCA (a group savings circle where members contribute and borrow), collective income projects, emergency fund.

## LEVEL 4 — Health Autonomy 
Community health worker, herbal garden, and basic sanitation (latrines, safe waste management) 

## LEVEL 5 — Energy Access 
Solar home systems, charging stations, improved cookstoves, biogas.

## LEVEL 6 — Education
Community learning space, local teachers, literacy for all.

## LEVEL 7 — Small Node
200–500 people with water, food, energy, health, education, governance.

**Partial Flow saves lives.**
