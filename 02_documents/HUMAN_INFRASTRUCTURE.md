## I. PURPOSE

Human Infrastructure (HI) defines the **systems, roles, and protocols** for the sustainable operation, development, and wellbeing of human communities in Flow. It ensures that:

- Every human has guaranteed Baseline access.  
- Roles and responsibilities are fairly distributed.  
- Knowledge, skills, and civic capacity circulate.  
- Power concentration is prevented while allowing coordination.  
- Community resilience is maintained under stress or emergencies.  

Human Infrastructure is **human-centric**: the human alone is the fundamental unit; all structures exist to support, circulate, and enhance human capacity.

---

## II. CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Baseline Security:** Every individual’s physiological and psychological needs are guaranteed.  
2. **Rotation:** All roles are temporary and periodically rotated to prevent power capture.  
3. **Participation:** Humans participate according to capacity and consent.  
4. **Transparency:** Roles, responsibilities, and decisions are visible to the community.  
5. **Deliberation:** Major human-system decisions are deliberative, evidence-informed, and bias-aware.  
6. **Resilience:** Systems adapt to environmental, social, and technological shocks.  
7. **Interdependence:** Human roles are interconnected; responsibilities are shared across scales.  

---

## III. SCALES OF HUMAN INFRASTRUCTURE

### A. Individual
- Ensures access to Baseline.  
- Develops skills and civic literacy.  
- Maintains autonomy while participating in community functions.  

### B. Circle / Community
- Localized human teams managing shared resources, knowledge, and coordination.  
- Direct participation in most local decisions.  
- Provides training, mentoring, and conflict resolution structures.  

### C. Node / Regional
- Cross-community coordination for infrastructure, crisis response, and logistics.  
- Roles filled via deterministic rotation or lottery.  
- Monitors resource flows, stress indicators, and wellbeing metrics.  

### D. Global Network
- Strategic human coordination for planetary-scale challenges.  
- Temporary panels selected with rotation rules.  
- Functions as advisory: ensures human-centered feedback in automated systems.  

---

## IV. ROLE CATEGORIES

1. **Care Roles** – health, education, mental wellbeing, caregiving.  
2. **Coordination Roles** – resource allocation, logistics, conflict mediation.  
3. **Knowledge Roles** – documentation, expertise sharing, skill propagation.  
4. **Crisis Roles** – emergency response, hazard mitigation, rapid deployment.  
5. **Observation Roles** – feedback loops, metrics collection, system monitoring.  

**All roles are subject to rotation, documentation, and community verification.**

---

## V. ROLE SELECTION AND ROTATION

- **Eligibility:** All individuals meeting minimum Baseline and training criteria.  
- **Selection:** Randomized and deterministic rotation to prevent bias and power concentration.  
- **Term Limits:** Roles are time-limited (30–90 days depending on scale).  
- **Cooldown:** Individuals cannot occupy the same role consecutively without a minimum break.  
- **Recusal:** Any individual with a conflict of interest may self-recuse; automatic logging required.  

---

## VI. DELIBERATION AND DECISION-MAKING

1. **Information Packets:** Distributed before deliberation; neutral, multi-perspective, evidence-based.  
2. **Protected Deliberation:** Internal discussion without external manipulation.  
3. **Open Consultation:** Public input and feedback phase.  
4. **Final Decision:** Panel finalizes action; voting and reasoning documented.  
5. **Bias Awareness:** Training on cognitive biases, groupthink, manipulation detection.  

---

## VII. DOCUMENTATION AND TRANSPARENCY

- **All human roles logged:** identity, term, responsibilities, decisions.  
- **Logs auditable** (locally and cryptographically if needed).  
- **Sensitive data anonymized**; activity and participation metrics remain transparent.  
- **Periodic review** of role effectiveness, rotation compliance, and human outcomes.  

---

## VIII. FAILURE MODES AND MITIGATION

1. **Role Capture:** Increase pool, enforce term limits.  
2. **Low Participation:** Improve Baseline support, training, cultural incentives.  
3. **Manipulation:** Protected deliberation, Red/Blue Team analysis.  
4. **Resource Imbalance:** Rapid resource reallocation, crisis deployment.  
5. **Knowledge Loss:** Mandatory documentation, mentoring, redundancy.  

---

## IX. INTEGRATION WITH AUTOMATION

- Human Infrastructure supports automated coordination but can **override when human judgment is critical**.  
- Provides feedback loops to RIS and AI systems.  
- Ensures humans remain central in decisions affecting wellbeing, ethics, or Baseline.  

---

## X. METRICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY

- **Participation Metrics:** hours served, role fulfillment, rotation adherence.  
- **Wellbeing Metrics:** Baseline coverage, health, mental health, education, satisfaction.  
- **Audit Reports:** Quarterly reviews of compliance, rotation, deliberation quality.  
- **Improvement Loops:** Failures trigger system updates and training.  

---

## XI. FINAL PRINCIPLE

> **“Human Infrastructure circulates knowledge, care, and responsibility through humans themselves. Power, roles, and decision-making must rotate; humans are the center of Flow.”**
