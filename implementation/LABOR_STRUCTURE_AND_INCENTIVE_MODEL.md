## 1. PURPOSE

This document defines how labor is structured, measured, distributed, and stabilized inside Flow.

It replaces wage-based labor incentives with:
- Time-based contribution accounting
- Rotational civic participation
- Specialist depth tracks
- Structured responsibility without material hierarchy

Labor in Flow is:
- Not monetized
- Not commodified
- Not tied to survival
- Required for systemic continuity

Baseline is unconditional.
Participation sustains the system.

---

## 2. CORE PRINCIPLE

Human value is equal.
Human responsibility is contextual.

All capable adults participate in maintaining shared infrastructure.

Time is used as a **logistical unit**, not as a measure of human worth.

---

## 3. BASELINE PARTICIPATION REQUIREMENT

### 3.1 Civic Contribution Threshold

Each capable adult contributes a minimum weekly number of hours to Node operations.

Example baseline:
- 6–12 hours per week
- Adjusted for health, caregiving, study, age

This is not employment.
It is distributed civic maintenance.

---

## 4. LABOR CATEGORIES

### 4.1 Core Infrastructure Roles
Water, food, energy, healthcare, sanitation, fabrication.

These must always be staffed.

### 4.2 Rotational Civic Roles
Sanitation, basic maintenance, public logistics, support functions.

- Rotated across population
- Short cycles
- Designed to prevent permanent low-status labor

### 4.3 Specialist Tracks

Specialists:
- Receive extended training (counted as contribution hours)
- Are exempt from some rotation requirements
- Hold higher responsibility within their domain
- Are peer-certified and periodically revalidated

Specialists do not receive:
- Increased material consumption
- Financial compensation
- Permanent political dominance

Depth replaces breadth.
Responsibility replaces privilege.

---

## 5. INCENTIVE STRUCTURE

Flow does not rely on monetary incentives.

It relies on:

### 5.1 Meaning
Work directly sustains visible community stability.

### 5.2 Status Through Competence
Respect is tied to skill mastery and reliability.

### 5.3 Autonomy
Specialists gain higher decision-weight inside their domain.

### 5.4 Role Protection
Highly trained individuals are protected from constant rotation burden.

---

## 6. UNDESIRABLE TASK DISTRIBUTION

No permanent low-status roles.

Mechanisms:
- Mandatory rotation pools
- Automation priority
- Short shift segmentation
- Large population distribution

If 10 undesirable hours/day exist in a 500-person Node:
→ 0.14 hours per person per week.

Scaling solves burden.

---

## 7. QUALITY & ACCOUNTABILITY

### 7.1 Peer Certification
Competence validated by domain experts.

### 7.2 Transparent Documentation
All critical systems documented and publicly auditable.

### 7.3 Named Responsibility Teams
Each module has visible human responsibility.

### 7.4 Systemic Failure Review
Errors analyzed structurally, not morally.

---

## 8. NON-PARTICIPATION PROTOCOL

Baseline is unconditional.

If chronic non-participation occurs:

1. Dialogue (health, mismatch, conflict)
2. Role reassignment
3. Temporary decision-weight limitation

Never:
- Removal of food
- Removal of shelter
- Removal of healthcare

Participation affects governance weight, not survival.

---

## 9. GOVERNANCE INTERFACE

Labor hours do NOT directly translate to political power.

However:
- Active participants retain full governance voice.
- Chronic disengagement may reduce decision influence.
- Domain expertise increases voice within that domain only.

Horizontal democracy + competence councils.

---

## 10. INTER-NODE LABOR TRANSFER

Hours are not traded.

People move voluntarily between Nodes.
Temporary specialist exchange allowed.
Digital consultation encouraged.

Labor is embodied, not abstract.

---

## 11. SYSTEM RISKS

Recognized risks:
- Social pressure escalation
- Informal hierarchy formation
- Specialist burnout
- Civic disengagement clusters

Mitigations:
- Rotation audits
- Psychological safety channels
- Periodic workload recalibration
- Transparent metrics

---

## 12. SUMMARY

Flow replaces:

Wage → Contribution  
Salary → Status through competence  
Job market → Civic rotation + specialization tracks  
Punishment → Governance-weight modulation  

Baseline remains inviolable.

System continuity depends on structured participation.
