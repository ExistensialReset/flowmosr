## 1. PURPOSE

This document defines the implementation plan for hydroponic food production within Flow Nodes. It specifies production types, Node-level coordination, regional exchange, and professional team integration.

---

## 2. NODE-LEVEL HYDROPONICS PRODUCTION

### 2.1 Core Principles
- **Baseline First:** Each Node guarantees automatic access to essential foods.
- **Local Production:** All primary production occurs locally in hydroponic systems.
- **Specialization:** Nodes can focus on complementary crops, algae, legumes, and protein-rich plants.
- **Sustainability:** Energy-efficient LED lighting, closed-loop water systems, and nutrient recycling.

### 2.2 Example Crop List
- Leafy greens: Lettuce, spinach, kale
- Herbs: Basil, mint, coriander
- Protein plants: Lentils, beans, chickpeas
- Algae: Spirulina, chlorella
- Fruit: Strawberries, tomatoes (small-scale)

### 2.3 Node Infrastructure
- **Hydroponic bays:** Modular racks with automated nutrient delivery
- **Water & nutrient tanks:** Centralized distribution with filtration and recycling
- **Lighting:** LED spectrum optimized for plant growth
- **Climate control:** Temperature and humidity regulation
- **Monitoring:** IoT sensors for pH, EC, nutrient levels, and growth metrics

---

## 3. REGIONAL COORDINATION

### 3.1 Exchange of Surplus
- **15% of produce** is allocated for regional redistribution.
- Nodes send excess crops to other Nodes with deficits.
- Coordination is handled by Regional Flow Hub (no currency, direct resource allocation).

### 3.2 Specialization Example
- Node A focuses on leafy greens and herbs  
- Node B focuses on protein plants and legumes  
- Node C grows algae and specialty crops  
- Regional hub balances distribution based on demand

---

## 4. PROFESSIONAL TEAM INTEGRATION
- **Agronomists & Hydroponic Technicians:** Plan crop rotation, nutrient cycles, and optimize yield
- **Maintenance Staff:** Ensure pumps, lights, and climate systems are operational
- **Community Training:** Lyceum workshops for Node members to learn hydroponic techniques
- **Peer Review:** Teams rotate across Nodes to share best practices

---

## 5. QUALITY ASSURANCE
- **Sensors & Monitoring:** Continuous automated checks on nutrient delivery and growth rates
- **Peer Oversight:** Teams audit other Nodes’ production for reliability and efficiency
- **Rotation & Feedback:** Annual review of crop yield and node performance

---

## 6. NON-CONTRIBUTING NODES OR MEMBERS
- Baseline remains guaranteed
- Soft social nudges encourage participation
- Knowledge sharing and community workshops provide low-barrier entry
