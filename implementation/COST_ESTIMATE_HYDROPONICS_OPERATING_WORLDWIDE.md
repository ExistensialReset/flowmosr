## 1. PURPOSE

This file estimates **recurring annual operating costs** for a Node‑level hydroponic system in different world regions. Includes energy, nutrients, labour, water, maintenance, and supplies.

---

> Electricity (often 20–30% of operating cost) is usually the biggest recurring expense, particularly where sunlight is limited and artificial lighting is used extensively16.

---

## 4. ENERGY & LABOUR INSIGHTS

1. **Energy costs:**  
   - LED lighting and climate systems dominate electricity use.  
   - Regions with expensive electricity (Europe, North America) have higher operating thresholds.  
2. **Labour:**  
   - Skilled labour for hydroponics is higher than basic agriculture labour in most regions.  
   - Automation can reduce hours but not eliminate via automation and monitoring costs.

---

## 5. SUMMARY

Hydroponic operating costs are non‑negligible and often exceed setup amortization for initial years:

- **Operating range (annual):** ~$47,000–$166,000 per Node.  
- **Regional variation:** High in developed regions (energy and labour), lower in developing regions (labour), balanced by potential renewable energy integration.
