## CORE PRINCIPLE

**Children are sovereign beings in development, not small adults or future workers.**

**Baseline is unconditional. Participation is voluntary. Play is contribution.**

---

## AGE-APPROPRIATE PARTICIPATION

| Age | Participation Type | Max Hours/Day* | Decision Power |
|-----|-------------------|---------------|----------------|
| **0-5** | Play only, presence, observation | None | Adults decide |
| **6-9** | Short tasks if child asks/accepts invitation | 30-45 min | Child chooses yes/no |
| **10-13** | Longer tasks, small roles, mentoring | 1-2 hours | Child chooses & can change mind |
| **14-17** | Apprenticeships, real work, governance voice | 4 hours** | High autonomy with adult support |

*Applies to **structured contribution**, not free play or flow states  
**Exception for self-directed passion projects with mentor oversight

---

## CHILD LABOR vs VOLUNTARY CONTRIBUTION

### ❌ NEVER PERMITTED (Child Labor)
- Required for Baseline
- Can't stop without consequences
- Excessive hours
- Dangerous without supervision
- Anxious/obligated
- Adult benefits more than child

### ✅ PERMITTED (Voluntary Contribution)
- Chosen freely
- Can stop anytime
- Age-appropriate hours
- Proper supervision
- Joyful/meaningful
- Baseline guaranteed regardless

---

## CRITICAL PROTECTIONS

### Baseline Always Secure
- Food, housing, healthcare, education, safety
- **NEVER conditional on contribution**
- **NEVER affected by behavior**

### Voluntary Participation
- Adults may invite, must accept "no" immediately
- Children can change their mind
- No guilt, shame, or pressure
- No comparison between children

### Voice in Decisions
- Children have say in decisions affecting them
- Children's Council for ages 10+ (recommended)
- Teens 14+ increasing governance participation

### Protection from Harm
- Mandatory reporting of abuse
- Follow HARM_BOUNDARY_PROTOCOL
- Children shielded from adult conflicts
- Immediate action if harm suspected

### Right to Leave
- Ages 16+ can leave Flow with Baseline support
- No guilt, coercion, or manipulation
- Open door to return

---

## RED FLAGS - INTERVENE IMMEDIATELY

🚨 **Child contributing out of fear** → Reassure Baseline, investigate pressure  
🚨 **Excessive hours or exhaustion** → Reduce immediately, rest required  
🚨 **Anxiety about not contributing enough** → Private conversation, permission to stop  
🚨 **Adult pressure or guilt** → Adult education, monitoring, possible role removal  
🚨 **Child discloses abuse** → Believe, document, report to authorities, ensure safety  
🚨 **Social pressure or comparison** → Circle-wide culture check, explicit permission to opt out

## NEURODIVERGENT CHILDREN

**Environment adapts, not child.**

### ADHD
- Short tasks or allow hyperfocus
- Frequent breaks, movement okay
- No punishment for distraction/forgetting

### Autism
- Predictable routines
- Sensory accommodations
- Clear, explicit communication
- Stimming welcomed

### PDA (Demand Avoidance)
- Reframe as choices, never demands
- Remove "should/have to" language
- High autonomy

### All Neurodivergence
- Individualized support
- Presume competence
- Celebrate differences

---

## ADOLESCENT-SPECIFIC TOPICS

### Sexuality & Relationships
- Comprehensive sex education (ages 14+)
- Condoms freely available (no shame, no monitoring)
- Consent education (enthusiastic, ongoing, revocable)
- Frontal lobe development explained (~25 years for full maturity)
- LGBTQ+ affirming

### Technology
- Age-appropriate screen time guidelines
- Education on algorithmic manipulation
- Privacy and online safety
- Balance with physical activity and socializing

### Leaving Flow
- Ages 16+ can leave with support
- Baseline assistance for transition
- No guilt or coercion to stay
- Can return if they change mind

## QUICK DECISION TREE

**Child wants to help with task:**
1. Is it safe for their age? → If no, adapt or decline
2. Can they stop anytime? → If no, make it stoppable
3. Is Baseline unconditional? → Always yes
4. Will this interfere with play/rest/learning? → If yes, reduce or reschedule
5. Is child joyful? → If no, pause and check in

**Proceed if all checks pass. Monitor and adjust.**

✅ **Prioritize play over productivity**  
✅ **Check in on emotional state**  
✅ **Consult full Playbook**  
✅ **Bring to Circle for discussion**
