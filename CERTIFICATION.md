## 📜 Purpose

This document defines the **criteria, process, and enforcement mechanisms** for certifying an implementation as a **Compliant Node** within the M-OS-R / Flow / Existential Reset ecosystem.

A Compliant Node:  
- Respects the anti-extraction principles  
- Follows ethical protocols  
- Maintains transparency and decentralization  
- May legally use the trademarks: M-OS-R, Flow, Existential Reset  

---

## ✅ Certification Criteria

### 1. Governance & Financial Rules
- [ ] No dividends or profit distribution to stakeholders  
- [ ] No capital-based governance or ownership stake  
- [ ] Any cost recovery is strictly limited to actual expenses  

### 2. Ethical & Cultural Compliance
- [ ] Stopping rules implemented for AI/systemic interventions  
- [ ] Local cultural calibration and human oversight  
- [ ] Anti-surveillance and data protection safeguards  

### 3. Anti-Centralization & Anti-Capture
- [ ] Transparent governance processes documented publicly  
- [ ] Rotation of key roles to prevent power concentration  
- [ ] No expert capture: critical decisions must involve multiple contributors  

### 4. No Gamification / Optimization of $\Sigma$
- [ ] No leaderboards, scoring systems, or ranking of participants  
- [ ] No attempts to optimize grace ($\Sigma$) outcomes or promise measurable ROI  

---

## 🛠️ Certification Process

1. **Self-Assessment:** Node implementer reviews criteria and completes the **Compliant Node Checklist**.  
2. **Documentation:** Submit architecture, governance rules, and ethical protocols for review.  
3. **Community Review:** Existing Compliant Node stewards verify adherence to criteria.  
4. **Certification Granted:** If all criteria are met, Node is added to the **official Compliant Node registry**.  

> Only nodes on this registry may publicly use the names **M-OS-R, Flow, or Existential Reset**.  

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement & Compliance

- **Ongoing Monitoring:** Compliant Nodes must provide annual updates on governance, ethical protocols, and financial practices.  
- **Revocation:** Certification may be revoked if a Node:  
  - Violates anti-profit rules  
  - Skips ethical or cultural safeguards  
  - Engages in gamification or monetization  
- **Fork & Restore:** If a certified Node is captured, community may fork the repository and restore compliance under a new name.
