## 1. Welcome

**Flow + GCCS = two layers of the same transition**  

- **GCCS:** Global Charter of Collective Stewardship – provides **resources, legal protection, planetary coordination**.  
- **Flow:** Local human-scale system – provides **daily life governance, care, conflict resolution, nervous-system-aware protocols**.

Flow can operate without GCCS; GCCS accelerates scaling.

| Layer | Purpose | Key Features |

## 3. Flow Node Integration (Simple)

### Option A — Individual Mode
- Keep UBIS personally  
- Voluntarily contribute time/resources  
- Node operates hybrid

### Option B — Pooling Mode (Recommended)
- Circle decides percentage of UBIS to pool  
- Node uses for collective infrastructure (land, food, housing, energy, tools)  
- Remaining UBIS is personal

### Option C — Full Commons Mode
- Pool 100% UBIS  
- Personal needs met through Node  
- High-trust, mature Nodes

**Rule:** Pooling is always voluntary. Exit is always possible.

---

## 4. Governance

**Flow Governance:**
- Circles (5-30 people)
- LOTUS sortition for rotation  
- Consensus preferred, majority acceptable  
- Conflict mediation & restorative practices

**GCCS Governance:**
- Global Governance Council, Global Judicial Tribunal, Global Commons Authority, Office of Global Auditor  
- Subsidiarity Shield protects local autonomy  
- Legal enforcement & anti-capture mechanisms

**Compatibility:** Flow Nodes operate as **locally sovereign units** under GCCS legal & resource framework.

---

## 5. Why Integration Matters

- **GCCS without Flow:** resources exist but communities may collapse, chaos risk  
- **Flow without GCCS:** small islands of functioning communities, limited scale  
- **Flow + GCCS:** material possibility + lived reality → scalable, ethical, resilient communities

## 6. Onboarding for New Participants

1. **Learn the basics:** Flow Circle + Node concepts  
2. **Join or start a Node:** 5–50 people weekly  
3. **Ratify GCCS Charter digitally** (optional but recommended)  
4. **Decide pooling mode:** voluntary, transparent  
5. **Follow Flow protocols:** LOTUS governance, conflict mediation, care infrastructure  
6. **Experiment locally:** collect data, iterate, fork if needed  

---

## 7. Key Principles to Remember

- Participation is **always voluntary**  
- Nodes are **forkable** (no monopoly on “correct” model)  
- Transparency is required (finances, decisions)  
- Subsidiarity respected (local autonomy first)  
- Exit rights guaranteed  

> **Flow = how to live together**  
> **GCCS = resources & legal protection**  
> Together → scalable, livable, ethical post-capitalist communities
