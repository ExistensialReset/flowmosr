## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Nothing About Us Without Us

**Disabled people make decisions about disability access.**
- Wheelchair users design wheelchair infrastructure.
- Autistic people design sensory protocols.

---

### 2. Universal Design (Built In, Not Bolted On)

- **Equitable Use:** Same means of use for all.
- **Physical Spaces:** Ramps on ALL buildings, wide pathways, tactile paving.
- **Communication:** Text AND audio, sign language, simple language versions.

---

### 3. Disability as Diversity (Not Deficit)

**Social model (FLOW):**
- Disability = mismatch between body and environment.
- Solution = change environment to accommodate body.

---

### 4. Access is Baseline (Not Luxury)

**Every person receives:**
- Mobility aids, sensory aids, communication devices, mental health support.
- If you need it to function, you get it. No gatekeeping.

---

### 5. Multiple Ways to Participate

- No single "right" way to engage.
- Meetings are hybrid, asynchronous options exist, and sensory-friendly spaces are standard.

---

### 6. Disability Justice

- **Intersectionality:** Addressing race, gender, and class within disability.
- **Sustainability:** Pacing work for disabled bodies. Rest as resistance.

---

### 7. Neurodivergent Inclusion

- Sensory-friendly lighting and quiet spaces.
- Direct communication valued (no "hints").
- Stimming normalized.

---

### 8. Mental Health as Disability

- **Refugium Anima (3-stage recovery):** Rest, Spontaneity, Reintegration.
- Mental illness is NEVER grounds for Baseline denial.
