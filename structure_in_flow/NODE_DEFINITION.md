# 🏘️ NODE_DEFINITION.md

**Purpose:** To clarify what different levels of Flow organization actually mean.  
**Problem:** People don't know if they're a "Circle," "Baseline Circle," or "Node."  
**Solution:** Clear definitions with concrete criteria.

---

## THE THREE LEVELS

Flow organization exists on a spectrum, not a cliff.

**You don't "become" a Node overnight.**  
**You grow through phases.**

---

## 📍 LEVEL 1: MICRO-CIRCLE (2-5 people)

### **Definition:**
Friends who share resources informally.

### **Duration:**
3 months - 1 year

### **Characteristics:**
✅ No formal structure  
✅ Irregular meetings (weekly or monthly)  
✅ Small resource sharing (meals, tools, transport)  
✅ Emotional support and conversation  
✅ Can dissolve anytime without ritual  

### **Economic model:**
- Mostly individual finances
- Occasional shared purchases
- Gift economy (no tracking)

### **Decision-making:**
- Informal consensus
- No written agreements
- "Let's try this" approach

### **Examples:**
- 3 friends who cook together every Sunday
- 5 neighbors who share a lawnmower and car
- 4 people who meet for weekly forest walks

### **What this is NOT:**
- A commune
- A legal entity
- A commitment for life

### **Exit:**
Just stop coming. No drama needed.

---

## 📍 LEVEL 2: BASELINE CIRCLE (10-30 people)

### **Definition:**
A group that collectively secures Baseline (food, shelter, healthcare, tools) for its members.

### **Duration:**
6 months - 2 years to stabilize

### **Characteristics:**
✅ Regular meetings (weekly or bi-weekly)  
✅ Economic coordination (one of 3 models)  
✅ Resource mapping (who has what, who needs what)  
✅ Written agreements (Sharing Boundaries, Power Inventory)  
✅ Conflict protocols (documented, practiced)  
✅ Exit/Compost protocols  

### **Economic models (choose one):**
1. **Pooled Fund** (full sharing)
2. **Split Bill** (proportional by income)
3. **Solidarity Network** (opt-in help)

### **Decision-making:**
- Consent-based or consensus
- Rotating facilitator
- Documented in shared ledger

### **Infrastructure:**
- Meeting space (rented, borrowed, or Studiecirkel via ABF in Sweden)
- Shared tools/resources
- Communication channel (Signal, shared doc)
- Transparency Ledger

### **Examples:**
- 15 people sharing food costs through bulk buying
- 20 people with shared tool library and childcare co-op
- 12 people pooling money for rent in collective housing

### **What this IS:**
- Serious commitment (but still voluntary)
- Structured mutual aid
- Economic resilience through collaboration

### **What this is NOT:**
- A full Node (not yet)
- Self-sufficient (still relies on external economy)
- Permanent (can dissolve gracefully)

### **Exit:**
Follow EXIT_AND_COMPOST.md protocol:
- 30-day notice
- Bridge Baseline (3 months support)
- Graceful departure ritual

---

## 📍 LEVEL 3: FLOW NODE (30+ people)

### **Definition:**
Full infrastructure for Existential Sovereignty.

A Node guarantees Baseline for all members through internal production, professional support, and inter-Node cooperation.

### **Duration:**
2-5 years to reach this level from zero

### **Characteristics:**
✅ **Professional Team** (healthcare, legal, technical specialists)  
✅ **Volunteer/Research Team** (mentors, artisans, researchers)  
✅ **Lyceum Musaeum** (physical or digital space for learning, workshops, library)  
✅ **Baseline Resource Access** for ALL members:
   - Food (local production + distribution)
   - Energy (solar, wind, or cooperative grid)
   - Tools (maker space, repair café)
   - Healthcare (on-site or coordinated)
✅ **Inter-Node cooperation** (part of regional network)  
✅ **Crisis resilience** (can survive 6 months without external economy)  
✅ **Conflict Mode & Node Sovereignty**: A Node can autonomously activate internal protocols to secure Baseline in case of threats to resources, safety, or life-sustaining functions. Priorities are documented and shared transparently via the Node Ledger and Lyceum.

### **Economic model:**
- Baseline guaranteed for all
- Surplus shared with regional network
- Minimal external currency dependency (30% or less)

### **Decision-making:**
- Sociocratic circles (working groups with clear mandates)
- Regional coordination via delegates
- Axiom-based governance (AXIOMS.md)

### **Infrastructure:**
- Land or buildings (owned, rented, or squatted)
- Production capacity (gardens, workshops, energy systems)
- Lyceum Musaeum (learning/making space)
- Professional support (nurses, counselors, legal advisors)

### **Examples:**
- 40 people living in collective housing with on-site garden, workshop, and healthcare
- 50 people across 3 buildings coordinating food production, childcare, and elder care
- 35 people on rural land with permaculture, solar power, and repair infrastructure

### **What this IS:**
- A functioning alternative to Mammon
- Existential Sovereignty in practice
- Proof that Flow works

### **What this is NOT:**
- Utopia (conflicts still happen)
- Permanent (Nodes can dissolve)
- Independent (needs regional network)

### **Exit:**
Follow EXIT_AND_COMPOST.md protocol:
- 90-day transition period
- Full Baseline support during transition
- Contribution to Node knowledge base
