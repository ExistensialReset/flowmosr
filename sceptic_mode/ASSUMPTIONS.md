# 🔍 ASSUMPTIONS
## What must be true for Flow to work

This system is not built on belief.

It is built on **assumptions**.

If enough of these are wrong, incomplete, or context-dependent →  
**Flow does not work as intended.**

---

## 🧱 1. MATERIAL ABUNDANCE EXISTS

We assume:

- Global food production can meet or exceed demand  
- Renewable energy can scale to cover essential infrastructure  
- Basic needs (food, shelter, water, energy, connectivity) are physically solvable  

This is the **foundation layer**.

Flow does not attempt to solve absolute scarcity.  
It assumes we are already past that threshold in many domains.

**If false:**
→ Allocation problems dominate  
→ Cooperation collapses into competition  
→ Flow becomes a rationing system  

**What would falsify this:**
- Persistent inability to meet basic needs despite optimal distribution  
- Resource constraints that cannot be engineered around  

---

## 🧠 2. HUMANS ARE CONTEXT-DEPENDENT

We do not assume humans are “good”.

We assume:

- Behavior adapts to environment and incentives  
- Scarcity + pressure → extraction and self-preservation  
- Safety + dignity → increased cooperation (not guaranteed, but probable)

Flow is designed around **behavioral plasticity**, not moral ideals.

**If false:**
→ Exploitation persists regardless of system design  
→ Free-riding dominates  
→ Trust networks fail  

**What would falsify this:**
- No measurable change in behavior after removing survival pressure  
- Persistent high exploitation even in safe conditions  

---

## 🔄 3. COORDINATION WITHOUT PRICE SIGNALS IS POSSIBLE

We assume:

- Needs can be made visible without money  
- People can respond to real needs without financial incentives  
- Local coordination + transparency can replace parts of market logic  

This does not mean “no structure”.  
It means **different coordination signals**.

**If false:**
→ Resource misallocation  
→ Bottlenecks and hidden shortages  
→ System inefficiency  

**What would falsify this:**
- Chronic mismatch between needs and contributions  
- Inability to coordinate even at small scales  

---

## 🛡️ 4. BASELINE SECURITY CHANGES MOTIVATION

We assume:

- When survival is guaranteed, motivation reorganizes  
- Contribution shifts toward meaning, care, curiosity, and social belonging  

We do **not** assume everyone contributes equally.

We assume enough people will contribute to sustain the system.

**If false:**
→ Contribution deficit  
→ System becomes unsustainable  

**What would falsify this:**
- Majority disengagement after baseline is secured  
- Critical roles consistently left unfilled  

---

## 🧩 5. SMALL-SCALE TRUST CAN SCALE VIA FEDERATION

We assume:

- Trust works best in small groups  
- Larger systems can emerge by linking small units (nodes)  
- Decentralization prevents power concentration  

This is the **anti-centralization hypothesis**.

**If false:**
→ Fragmentation (systems don’t connect)  
→ Or centralization re-emerges (power concentrates again)  

**What would falsify this:**
- Failure to coordinate across nodes  
- Emergence of dominant hubs controlling the system  

---

## ⚖️ 6. GOVERNANCE CAN RESIST CAPTURE (LOTUS)

We assume:

- Sortition reduces long-term power accumulation  
- Time limits reduce corruption  
- Constitutional constraints protect core principles  

We do not assume perfect governance.  
We assume **less capture than existing systems**.

**If false:**
→ Power consolidates anyway  
→ Informal elites dominate decisions  

**What would falsify this:**
- Repeated capture patterns despite rotation  
- Decision-making consistently influenced by hidden actors  

---

## 🧍 7. PEOPLE WILL TOLERATE IMPERFECTION

Flow is not optimized for efficiency.

We assume:

- People will accept some inefficiency in exchange for dignity and autonomy  
- Not everything needs to be optimized  

**If false:**
→ Pressure to reintroduce optimization systems  
→ Drift back toward extractive logic  

**What would falsify this:**
- Strong demand for efficiency over wellbeing  
- Reintroduction of coercive productivity norms  

---

## 🌍 8. CULTURAL ADAPTABILITY IS POSSIBLE

We assume:

- Flow can adapt to different cultural contexts  
- It is not a one-size-fits-all system  

**If false:**
→ Works locally, fails globally  
→ Becomes culturally incompatible in many regions  

**What would falsify this:**
- Repeated failure across different social contexts  
- High resistance to core principles  

---

## 🧠 FINAL NOTE

These assumptions are not hidden.

They are **explicit and testable**.

If you disagree with them:

Good.

That’s where the real work begins.
