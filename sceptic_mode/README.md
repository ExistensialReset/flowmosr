We assume this might fail.

So here it is — clearly, without hiding anything:

- What must be true

---

## 🔍 1. ASSUMPTIONS
### (What must be true for this to work)

These are not opinions.  
These are **dependencies**.

If enough of these are wrong → Flow does not work.

---

### 1.1 Material Abundance Exists

We assume:

- Food production can exceed global demand
- Energy can scale via renewables
- Basic infrastructure can be widely accessible

**If false:**
→ Flow collapses into scarcity allocation  
→ Conflict replaces cooperation  

---

### 1.2 Humans Are Context-Dependent

We do **not** assume humans are “good”.

We assume:

- Behavior adapts to incentives and environment
- Remove survival pressure → behavior changes
- Add extraction incentives → exploitation emerges

**If false:**
→ Free-riding and exploitation dominate  
→ System degrades quickly  

---

### 1.3 Coordination Without Money Is Possible

We assume:

- Needs can be visible without price signals
- People will respond to real needs when not threatened
- Local coordination can replace market abstraction (at least partially)

**If false:**
→ Resource misallocation  
→ Hidden shortages  
→ Inefficiency collapse  

---

### 1.4 Baseline Security Changes Motivation

We assume:

- When survival is guaranteed, people don’t stop contributing  
- Motivation reorganizes (meaning, care, curiosity)

**If false:**
→ Contribution drops below sustainability threshold  
→ System becomes parasitic  

---

### 1.5 Small Systems Can Scale via Federation

We assume:

- Trust works locally
- Larger systems emerge through networked nodes (not centralization)

**If false:**
→ Fragmentation OR power recentralization  

---

## ⚠️ 2. FAILURE MODES
### (How this breaks in reality)

This is where things go wrong.

Not theoretically.  
In practice.

---

### 🧠 Human Behavior Failures

**Free-riding beyond tolerance**
- Some people will contribute nothing
- Question is: how many before collapse?

**Social loafing in groups**
- Responsibility diffuses
- “Someone else will do it”

**Status hierarchies re-emerge**
- Informal power structures form anyway
- Charisma > structure

**Burnout in high contributors**
- Some people carry too much
- System silently depends on them

---

### 🏛️ Governance Failures (LOTUS)

**Sortition gaming**
- Groups coordinate to increase selection probability

**Shadow leadership**
- Real power moves outside formal roles

**Axiom rigidity**
- “Unchangeable” principles block necessary adaptation

---

### ⚙️ Operational Failures

**Supply chain fragility**
- Medicine, semiconductors, complex systems
- Hard to localize fully

**Coordination overhead**
- Scaling introduces friction
- More people ≠ smoother system

**Invisible labor imbalance**
- Care work may become unevenly distributed

---

### 🌍 External Pressure

**State resistance**
- Legal, regulatory, or direct suppression

**Market absorption**
- Flow gets repackaged and sold back

**Cultural mismatch**
- Works in some contexts, fails in others

---

## 🧨 3. WHERE THIS LIKELY BREAKS FIRST

Not hypothetically.

Real weak points.

---

### 3.1 The Transition Phase

This is the most dangerous part.

People are still dependent on:
- Money
- Jobs
- Legacy systems

Flow exists **on top of stress**, not instead of it.

→ Burnout risk  
→ Fragmentation risk  

---

### 3.2 Scaling Trust

Small groups work.

Large systems?
- Trust weakens
- Coordination cost explodes

This is a known problem. Not solved.

---

### 3.3 Motivation Mismatch

Not everyone will:
- Care
- Engage
- Contribute

Even with safety guaranteed.

→ Unknown tipping point  

---

### 3.4 Complex Infrastructure Dependency

Some systems are hard to decentralize:

- Healthcare
- Pharmaceuticals
- Advanced manufacturing

Flow may depend on legacy systems longer than expected.

---

### 3.5 Cultural Reality

This is not universal.

Some environments will reject:
- Non-transactional logic
- Voluntary contribution
- Decentralized authority

---

## 🔬 4. WHAT WOULD DISPROVE FLOW?

This is not unfalsifiable.

It can fail.

Clear signals:

- Contribution rates collapse despite baseline security  
- Persistent inequality emerges without monetary systems  
- Governance capture occurs despite LOTUS  
- Critical infrastructure cannot be maintained  
- Trust fails beyond small groups  

If these happen consistently:

→ Flow is incomplete or incorrect
