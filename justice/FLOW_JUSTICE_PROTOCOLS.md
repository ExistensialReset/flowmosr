## PURPOSE

This document defines the **operational implementation of Justice in Flow**.

While Principles define *what must always be true*, this document defines:

- How justice processes are executed  
- How roles function in practice  
- How cases move through the system  
- How edge cases and instability are handled  

---

## I. CASE FLOW (END-TO-END)

---

### 1. ENTRY POINTS

A case can begin through:

- Direct interpersonal conflict  
- Third-party observation  
- Safe Reporting Channel (SRC)  
- Pattern detection (system-level signals)  

---

### 2. INITIAL CLASSIFICATION

Every case is quickly classified:

- **Level 1 — Social Tension**
- **Level 2 — Active Harm / Conflict**
- **Level 3 — Severe / Repeated Harm**

---

### 3. SAFETY CHECK (MANDATORY)

Before any process:

- Assess perceived safety  
- If unsafe → activate **SOP (Safety Override Protocol)**  

---

### 4. LAYER ASSIGNMENT

| Level | Layer |
|------|------|
| Level 1 | Social Resolution |
| Level 2 | Structured Restoration |
| Level 3 | Formal Review (LOTUS) |

---

### 5. PROCESS EXECUTION

Each layer follows defined protocols (see below)

---

### 6. RESOLUTION + AGREEMENT

- Agreements documented (lightweight)  
- Follow-up defined if needed  

---

### 7. REFLECTION PHASE

- Trigger **Perceived Fairness Protocol (PFP)**  
- Capture system-level learnings  

---

### 8. CLOSURE

Case is closed when:

- Safety restored  
- Participants heard  
- No active escalation  

---

## II. PROCESS LAYERS

---

### LAYER 1 — SOCIAL RESOLUTION

**Use for:**
- Misunderstandings  
- Low-intensity conflict  
- Early-stage tension  

---

#### Flow:

1. Direct conversation encouraged  
2. Optional mediator  
3. No formal record  

---

#### Constraints:

- Must not be forced  
- Must not override safety  

---

### LAYER 2 — STRUCTURED RESTORATION

**Use for:**
- Clear harm  
- Emotional escalation  
- Failed Layer 1 attempts  

---

#### Session Structure:

1. Opening (ground rules, safety check)  
2. Individual sharing (structured turns)  
3. Reflection (Human Mirror if present)  
4. Harm articulation  
5. Needs identification  
6. Agreement creation  

---

#### Rules:

- No interruptions  
- Time-balanced speaking  
- Facilitator enforces structure  

---

### LAYER 3 — FORMAL REVIEW (LOTUS)

**Use for:**
- Severe harm  
- Repeated incidents  
- System-level impact  

---

#### Structure:

- Multi-person panel  
- Cross-cluster composition  
- Optional external observers  

---

#### Steps:

1. Case presentation (multi-perspective)  
2. Structured questioning  
3. Deliberation (facilitated)  
4. Outcome definition  

---

#### Output:

- Structural actions (not punishment)  
- System adjustments if needed  

---

## III. ROLES (OPERATIONAL)

---

### FACILITATOR

- Maintains structure  
- Enforces speaking order  
- Protects fairness and safety  

---

### HUMAN MIRROR

- Reflects emotional dynamics  
- Identifies misalignment  
- Helps participants feel seen  

---

### CHILD WITNESS (WHEN RELEVANT)

- Represents vulnerable perspective  
- Ensures impact on dependents is considered  

---

### OBSERVER (OPTIONAL)

- Monitors fairness  
- Can trigger process review  

---

## IV. CORE PROTOCOL ACTIVATIONS

---

### 4.1 SAFETY OVERRIDE PROTOCOL (SOP)

Activated immediately when:

- Someone feels unsafe  
- Harm is ongoing  

**Actions:**
- Separation  
- Activity restriction  
- Support allocation  

---

### 4.2 PERCEIVED FAIRNESS PROTOCOL (PFP)

After resolution:

- Ask all participants:
  “Was this process fair?”

If not:
- Trigger process review  

---

### 4.3 MANIPULATION RESILIENCE (MRP)

During all structured processes:

- Speaking order enforced  
- Time limits applied  
- Optional anonymized input  

---

### 4.4 REPEATED HARM ESCALATION (RHE)

Trigger:
- 3+ similar incidents / 6 months  

Action:
- Pattern-based review  
- Expanded panel  
- Structural measures  

---

### 4.5 SAFE REPORTING CHANNEL (SRC)

- Confidential input allowed  
- No requirement for formal accusation  

Used for:
- Early signals  
- Pattern detection  

---

### 4.6 RESTORATIVE BOUNDARY PROTOCOL (RBP)

Activated when:

- Harm persists  
- Refusal to engage  
- Severe breach  

Action:
- Protective measures  
- External review  
- Parallel processes  

---

4.7 INFORMAL POWER ACCUMULATION PROTOCOL (IPAP)

Purpose:
- Detect and mitigate long-term accumulation of informal influence.

Signals:
- Same individual dominates multiple processes
- Others defer consistently
- Reduced participation diversity
- Repeated central role presence

Response:
- Mandatory role rotation
- Temporary exclusion from facilitation roles
- Increased anonymization in input
- Expanded panel composition
Principle:
- Influence must circulate, or it becomes power.

## V. AGREEMENT STRUCTURE

---

Agreements must be:

- Clear  
- Time-bounded (if relevant)  
- Voluntary (within structural limits)  

---

### Types:

- Behavioral adjustments  
- Participation boundaries  
- Support requirements  
- Role modifications  

---

## VI. PATTERN DETECTION

---

System monitors:

- Repeated involvement  
- Reporting clusters  
- Silence after incidents  

---

### Signals:

- Drop in reporting  
- Same individuals recurring  
- Avoidance behavior  

### Response:

- Soft review  
- Possible escalation  

---

## VII. LOAD & CAPACITY MANAGEMENT

---

Justice work is high-load.

Rules:

- Rotation required  
- Recovery time mandatory  
- No sustained high emotional load  

---

### If overload detected:

- Reduce case intake speed  
- Expand facilitator pool  
- Activate external support  

---

## VIII. FAILURE MODES & RESPONSES

---

### 1. UNDER-REPORTING

**Signal:**
- Silence despite tension  

**Response:**
- Reinforce SRC  
- Increase trust-building  

---

### 2. OVERLOAD

**Signal:**
- Burnout in facilitators  

**Response:**
- Reduce throughput  
- Add capacity  

---

### 3. TRUST COLLAPSE

**Signal:**
- Participants disengage  
- Outcomes rejected  

**Response:**
- External review  
- Transparency increase  
- Process redesign  

---

### 4. SYSTEM GAMING

**Signal:**
- Strategic reporting  
- Narrative manipulation  

**Response:**
- Activate MRP  
- Expand panel  
- Use anonymization  

---

## IX. INTEGRATION POINTS

---
